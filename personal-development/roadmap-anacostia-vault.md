### **Roadmap for the Anacostia Vault**

#### **Introduction**

The Obsidian "Anacostia" vault is envisioned as a comprehensive knowledge base exploring the intersection of artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning (ML), and Africana studies, with a particular emphasis on bias detection and ethical considerations. This roadmap and game plan outline a structured approach to realizing your vision, ensuring the vault serves as a robust resource for personal research, collaboration, and teaching, while addressing current challenges and planning for future expansion.

---

#### **Scope and Goals**

The mission of the "Anacostia" vault is to create a dynamic, interconnected collection of notes that examines how AI and ML intersect with Africana studies, focusing on identifying and mitigating biases in AI systems that impact marginalized communities.

**Key Topics:**

- **AI and Machine Learning Fundamentals:** Concepts, algorithms, and frameworks.
- **Bias in AI:** Detection, measurement, and mitigation strategies.
- **Ethical Considerations in AI Development:** Policy implications and regulations.
- **Africana Studies and Technology:** Bias in AI affecting African American communities.
- **Case Studies and Real-World Applications:** Criminal justice, healthcare, education, and finance.

---

#### **Organization of Content**

To ensure a structured and navigable vault, we’ll organize notes into a hierarchical structure:

- **Foundations**
    - Machine Learning Algorithms: Neural networks, decision trees, etc.
    - African American History and Culture: Historical context for bias analysis.
- **Bias in AI**
    - Types of Bias: Statistical, structural, systemic.
    - Fairness Metrics: Statistical Parity Difference, Disparate Impact Ratio.
    - Mitigation Techniques: Preprocessing, algorithmic constraints, post-processing.
- **Ethics and Policy**
    - Ethical Principles: Fairness, accountability, transparency.
    - Legal Frameworks: GDPR, AI Act, U.S. regulations.
    - Corporate Responsibility: Industry standards and ethics boards.
- **Case Studies**
    - Criminal Justice: Predictive policing and sentencing algorithms.
    - Healthcare: AI diagnostics and racial bias in medical data.
    - Education: AI in grading and access disparities.
    - Finance: Credit scoring and loan approval biases.
- **Resources and References**
    - Books: Summaries and reviews (e.g., _Algorithms of Oppression_ by Safiya Umoja Noble).
    - Papers: AI ethics and bias research.
    - Websites: AI fairness tools and Africana studies resources.
    - Courses: Online courses (MIT OpenCourseWare, Coursera, etc.).

---

#### **System for Note Creation and Maintenance**

- **Templating System:** Standardized templates (e.g., `ai-ml-template.md`) using Obsidian’s Templater plugin.
- **Automated Backlinks:** Enhance templates with dynamic backlinking for interconnectivity.
- **Regular Updates:** Weekly reviews for updating content, fixing errors, and expanding research.
- **Community Contributions:** Guidelines for collaboration, submission, and formatting.

#### **Ensuring Connectivity and Interactivity**
