# Handout E: The Hypocrisy of American Slavery (1852) by Frederick Douglass 🦅🔥💜

✊🏿 **Overview:**  
In this seminal handout, Frederick Douglass unleashes his fiery indictment of American hypocrisy regarding freedom. With piercing eloquence and decolonial precision, Douglass asks: *"What, to the American slave, is your 4th of July?"* He reveals a nation’s celebration of liberty that leaves its enslaved people in a state of profound injustice—a truth that echoes with both historical weight and Africana resistance. This document is the cream of our Anacostia vault's crop, a roaring testament to the power of words, truth, and relentless dignity.  

---

## Key Excerpts 🎤📜

> **"Fellow-citizens, pardon me, allow me to ask, why am I called upon to speak here to-day? What have I, or those I represent, to do with your national independence? Are the great principles of political freedom and of natural justice, embodied in that Declaration of Independence, extended to us? I say it with a sad sense of the disparity between us. I am not included within the pale of glorious anniversary! Your high independence only reveals the immeasurable distance between us."**

> **"What, to the American slave, is your 4th of July? I answer; a day that reveals to him, more than all other days in the year, the gross injustice and cruelty to which he is the constant victim. To him, your celebration is a sham; your boasted liberty, an unholy license; your national greatness, swelling vanity..."**

These searing lines capture the essence of Douglass’s brutal critique—the stark contrast between the celebrated ideals of liberty and the lived reality of enslaved people. 🦅🔥

  Douglass exposes the **immense disparity** between American ideals and the reality of slavery. His words peel back the facade of “celebration” to reveal a system steeped in oppression.  
  *Decolonial Insight:* This critique is a radical reappropriation of the nation’s founding documents, challenging Eurocentric narratives and demanding that the voices of the oppressed be heard.

## Vocabulary & Key Concepts 📚✨

- **Embodied:** Made real; given tangible form.
- **Sham:** Fake; not genuine.
- **Denunciation:** A strong public statement declaring something is wrong.
- **Tyrant:** A ruler who exercises power cruelly.
- **Impudence:** Insolence; a lack of respect.
- **Solemnity:** Seriousness, formality.
- **Bombast:** Inflated, boastful speech.
- **Deception:** Misleading behavior or information.
- **Impiety:** A lack of reverence or religious respect.
- **Hypocrisy:** Pretense of having beliefs or virtues that one does not actually possess.
