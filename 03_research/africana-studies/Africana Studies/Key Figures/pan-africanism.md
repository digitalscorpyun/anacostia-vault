## **Context & Background 🌍**  
Pan-Africanism is a transnational and global movement aimed at fostering unity, solidarity, and political liberation among African people and their descendants across the world. It emerged in response to European colonialism, slavery, and racial oppression.

---

## **Main Points & Analysis 📌**  

### 🔥 **Historical Origins**  
- Rooted in the struggles against the transatlantic slave trade and colonialism.  
- Early proponents include Henry Sylvester Williams (Pan-African Conference, 1900) and W.E.B. Du Bois.  
- The concept grew as African nations fought for independence throughout the 20th century.  

---

### 🌍 **Key Figures**  
- **Marcus Garvey**: Advocated for Black nationalism and economic self-sufficiency through the Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA).  
- **W.E.B. Du Bois**: Intellectual advocate for African unity, organizing multiple Pan-African Congresses.  
- **Kwame Nkrumah**: Led Ghana’s independence and championed African unity and socialism.  
- **Julius Nyerere**: Promoted Ujamaa (African socialism) in Tanzania and broader continental cooperation.  

---

### 📅 **Major Pan-African Conferences & Milestones**  
- **1900 Pan-African Conference (London)**: Organized by Henry Sylvester Williams, promoting unity and anti-colonialism.  
- **Pan-African Congresses (1919 - 1945)**: Led by W.E.B. Du Bois, aimed at opposing colonial rule and promoting racial equality.  
- **1958 Accra Conference (Ghana)**: First meeting of independent African states, hosted by Kwame Nkrumah.  
- **1963 Formation of the Organization of African Unity (OAU)**: Precursor to the African Union (AU), promoting political and economic cooperation.  

---

### ✊🏾 **Contemporary Pan-Africanism**  
- African Union (AU): Successor to the OAU, promoting political unity, economic growth, and continental development.  
- Afrobeat & Cultural Movements: Figures like Fela Kuti expressed Pan-Africanist ideals through music.  
- Diaspora Advocacy: African-Americans, Caribbeans, and Afro-Latinos continue to engage with Pan-Africanist ideologies.

## **Competing Perspectives 🔄**  
- **Integration vs. Separation**: Debates persist over whether unity should be cultural, political, or economic.  
- **Global vs. Local Focus**: Some advocate for focusing on local issues before broader Pan-African unity.
