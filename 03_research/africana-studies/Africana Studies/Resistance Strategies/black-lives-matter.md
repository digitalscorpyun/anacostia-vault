# Black Lives Matter: A Movement for Racial Justice

## Overview
The Black Lives Matter (BLM) movement is a decentralized, global network of activists and organizations dedicated to eradicating systemic racism and advocating for Black liberation. This note examines the movement's origins, key principles, and its role in contemporary struggles for racial justice.

## Historical Context
- **Origins**: BLM was founded in 2013 in response to the acquittal of Trayvon Martin's killer.
- **Growth**: The movement gained momentum with protests against police brutality and racial injustice.
- **Philosophy**: Rooted in the belief that Black lives are inherently valuable and deserve respect and dignity.

## Key Figures and Organizations
- **Founders**: Alicia Garza, Patrisse Cullors, and Opal Tometi.
- **Influential Leaders**: John Lewis, Angela Davis, and Frederick Douglass.
- **Organizations**: Black Lives Matter Global Network, NAACP, and local chapters across the globe.

## Core Principles
- **Black Lives Matter Principles**: Empathy, justice, liberation, and collective value.
- **Decolonial Framework**: Challenges systemic racism and colonial legacies in institutions.
- **Intersectionality**: Recognizes the interconnected nature of race, gender, class, and other forms of oppression.

## Modern Impact
- **Policy Changes**: Advocacy for police reform and accountability.
- **Cultural Shift**: Increased awareness and dialogue about systemic racism.
- **Global Influence**: Inspired similar movements worldwide addressing racial and social injustice.
