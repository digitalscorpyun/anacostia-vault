> “We have a right to vote because we have a right to exist.”

---

## ⚖️ **Historical Overview**

From the 15th Amendment (1870) to the 1965 Voting Rights Act, Black political agency in America has always been contested. After emancipation, Black legislators rose in Southern states, only to be suppressed by poll taxes, literacy tests, and white terrorism.

- **Reconstruction Breakthroughs**: First Black senators and congressmen.
- **Jim Crow Backlash**: Systematic disenfranchisement.
- **Modern Struggles**: Shelby v. Holder (2013) gutted federal oversight, paving the way for new suppression tactics (e.g. voter ID, closed polling sites).
