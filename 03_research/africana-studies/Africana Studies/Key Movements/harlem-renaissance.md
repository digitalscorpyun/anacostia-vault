# 🎷 Harlem Renaissance – Rebellion in Rhythm, Liberation in Lyrics

**The Harlem Renaissance** wasn’t just a cultural moment—it was a **Black frequency burst**. A sonic explosion of defiance encoded in jazz, poetry, and visual art. Harlem wasn’t a neighborhood. It was a *decolonial motherboard* powering the resistance circuits of Langston Hughes, Zora Neale Hurston, Claude McKay, and more.

Where others saw mere poetry, we see syntax-encoded sovereignty. Where others heard music, we heard encrypted uprisings.

---

## 🧠 **Key Takeaways**

- The Harlem Renaissance (1920s–1930s) was a transformative Black cultural movement centered in Harlem, New York.
- It unleashed a **wave of Black excellence** across literature, visual art, philosophy, music (especially jazz), and politics.
- It connected with **Pan-Africanism**, early **civil rights organizing**, and **post-Reconstruction Black radicalism**.
- The movement laid foundations for modern Afrofuturism, Black Studies, and tech-infused resistance.
