# 🔍 Computational Justice

Computational Justice is the guiding framework that fuses data science, algorithmic audits, and decolonial theory to expose and dismantle biases within digital systems. In an era where AI shapes our futures, computational justice challenges the status quo by demanding transparency, accountability, and fairness from every algorithm.

---

## 📊 Core Principles

- **Algorithmic Fairness:**  
  - **Aim:** Ensure that AI systems deliver equitable outcomes using fairness metrics such as demographic parity and equalized odds.  
  - **Tools:** Leverage toolkits like IBM AI Fairness 360, Fairlearn, and custom scripts (e.g., [[bias-flag.py]]).

- **Data Scrutiny & Auditing:**  
  - **Objective:** Analyze training data critically to uncover historical and systemic biases that skew outcomes.  
  - **Techniques:** Employ statistical analysis, adversarial testing, and visual data audits.

- **Transparency & Accountability:**  
  - **Focus:** Demystify AI decision-making processes by thoroughly documenting algorithm workflows.  
  - **Implementation:** Develop robust feedback mechanisms to allow affected communities to challenge and correct biased outputs.

- **Decolonial & Interdisciplinary Approach:**  
  - **Vision:** Integrate Africana epistemology and decolonial critiques to unearth and challenge colonial biases embedded in data and models.  
  - **Impact:** Combine technical rigor with ethical, community-oriented reflections to reshape AI systems toward justice.

---

## 🧠 Why It Matters

Computational Justice is essential for creating AI systems that do not replicate or amplify historical injustices. By interrogating data and algorithmic processes through a decolonial lens, this framework:  
- Protects marginalized communities from unjust outcomes.  
- Ensures ethical principles guide technological innovation.  
- Reframes technical audits as part of a broader struggle for social and political equity.
