# Philosophy Roadmap

## 📌 Overview

The Philosophy Roadmap explores foundational and emerging philosophical ideas that intersect with Africana epistemology, AI fairness, algorithmic justice, and computational ethics. It integrates thinkers from diverse traditions, especially those whose works are rooted in decolonial, abolitionist, and relational frameworks.

## 🏷️ Major Sections

1. **Negritude and Decolonization** (Aimé Césaire)
    
2. **Intersectionality and Abolitionism** (Angela Davis)
    
3. **Relation and Creolization** (Édouard Glissant)
    

## 🏆 Key Figures

### Aimé Césaire

- **Philosophies:** Negritude, Decolonization, Cultural Resistance.
    
- **Relevance to AI Fairness:** Césaire’s critique of colonialism and advocacy for cultural identity aligns with decolonial approaches to AI, especially in resisting Eurocentric biases in algorithms.
    
- **Related Notes:** [[africana-studies-overview]], [[computational-justice]].
    

### Angela Davis

- **Philosophies:** Intersectionality, Abolitionism, Structural Analysis.
    
- **Relevance to AI Fairness:** Davis’s work on systemic oppression and abolition can inform frameworks aimed at dismantling oppressive algorithms and promoting ethical AI governance.
    
- **Related Notes:** [[computational-justice]], [[ubuntu-and-logic-design]].
    

### Édouard Glissant

- **Philosophies:** Relation, Creolization, Opacity, Decolonial Thought.
    
- **Relevance to AI Fairness:** Glissant’s concept of opacity challenges transparency models that prioritize control over understanding, advocating for the right to be unknowable and resisting algorithmic reductionism.
    
- **Related Notes:** [[africana-studies-overview]], [[ubuntu-and-logic-design]].
    

## 🌟 Impact & Legacy

The integration of these philosophical perspectives contributes to a richer, more inclusive framework for understanding AI fairness and algorithmic justice. By centering Africana epistemology, this roadmap challenges dominant paradigms and provides alternative models rooted in cultural resilience and communal ethics.
