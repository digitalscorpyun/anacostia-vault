# **Africana Studies - W.E.B. Du Bois**

> _"The problem of the twentieth century is the problem of the color line." — W.E.B. Du Bois_

---

## 📌 **Overview**

**W.E.B. Du Bois** (February 23, 1868 – August 27, 1963) was a pioneering African American **scholar, sociologist, historian, and civil rights activist**. One of the most influential intellectuals of the 20th century, he was a **leading voice for racial justice, Pan-Africanism, and Black empowerment**.

His contributions include the development of **double-consciousness**, the concept of **the-color-line**, and the theory of **the-talented-tenth**. As a co-founder of the **national-association-for-the-advancement-of-colored-people (naacp)**, Du Bois played a critical role in shaping modern civil rights movements. His scholarship laid the groundwork for **africana-studies**, challenging racist historical narratives and advocating for **global-black-unity**.

## 📜 **Historical Foundations**

- **double-consciousness** – The internal conflict of African Americans in a racist society.
- **the-color-line** – The central issue of racial division in the 20th century.
- **the-talented-tenth** – The belief that a highly educated Black elite should lead racial uplift.
- **the-niagara-movement** – A forerunner to the NAACP that advocated for full civil rights.

## ✊🏾 **Key Contributions & Movements**

### **intellectual-thought-and-activism**

- **the-souls-of-black-folk** (1903) – A foundational work introducing double-consciousness.
- **black-reconstruction-in-america** (1935) – A radical rewriting of Reconstruction history.
- **pan-african-congresses** – Advocated for global Black unity and African liberation.
- **editor-of-the-crisis** – Used the NAACP’s magazine to shape public opinion.

### **cultural-and-artistic-impact**

- **advocate-for-black-literature** – Encouraged the Harlem Renaissance’s intellectual boom.
- **historical-revisionism** – Debunked myths of Black inferiority in academia.
- **influence-on-afrofuturism** – His visions of Black self-determination inspired later generations.

### **political-and-social-change**

- **co-founded-the-naacp** – A pivotal organization in the Civil Rights Movement.
- **advocate-for-anti-colonialism** – Supported African independence movements.
- **exile-in-ghana** – Renounced U.S. citizenship and became a Ghanaian citizen.
