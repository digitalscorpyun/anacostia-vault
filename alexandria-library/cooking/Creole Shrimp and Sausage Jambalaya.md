**Servings**: 4  
**Prep Time**: 15 minutes  
**Cook Time**: 35 minutes

### **Ingredients**

- 1 cup **rice**
- 1/2 pound **shrimp**, peeled and deveined
- 1/2 pound **smoked sausage**, sliced
- 1 medium **onion**, diced
- 1 **green bell pepper**, diced
- 2 stalks **celery**, diced
- 2 cloves **garlic**, minced
- 1 can (14 oz) **diced tomatoes** (substitute with mixed vegetables if preferred)
- 2 cups **chicken broth**
- 1 teaspoon **Creole seasoning** (or to taste)
- 1/2 teaspoon **paprika**
- 1/2 teaspoon **dried thyme**
- 1/2 teaspoon **cayenne pepper** (optional for heat)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 2 tablespoons **olive oil** or **butter**
- Chopped **green onions** or parsley for garnish

### **Instructions**

1. **Sauté the Sausage and Vegetables**:
    
    - Heat olive oil or butter in a large skillet or pot over medium heat.
    - Add sliced smoked sausage and cook until browned, about 3-4 minutes. Remove and set aside.
    - In the same pot, sauté onions, bell pepper, celery, and garlic until softened, about 5 minutes.
2. **Season and Add Shrimp**:
    
    - Stir in the shrimp and cook until they turn pink, about 2-3 minutes. Remove and set aside with the sausage.
3. **Combine Ingredients**:
    
    - To the pot, add the diced tomatoes, chicken broth, rice, and seasonings (Creole seasoning, paprika, thyme, cayenne, salt, and pepper). Stir to combine.
4. **Simmer the Jambalaya**:
    
    - Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking, until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
5. **Add Back Shrimp and Sausage**:
    
    - Stir the cooked shrimp and sausage back into the pot during the last 5 minutes of cooking. Allow everything to heat through.
6. **Serve and Garnish**:
    
    - Remove from heat and let the jambalaya rest for 5 minutes. Garnish with chopped green onions or parsley.
