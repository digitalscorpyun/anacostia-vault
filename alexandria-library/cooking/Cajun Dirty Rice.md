### **Cajun Dirty Rice Recipe**

**Servings:** 4-6  
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes  
**Cook Time:** 30 minutes  
**Total Time:** 45 minutes

---

#### **Ingredients:**

- **1 cup long-grain white rice (uncooked)**
- **2 cups chicken broth (or water)**
- **1 tbsp olive oil**
- **1/2 lb ground beef or pork sausage (or a mix)**
- **1/2 lb chicken livers (optional, finely chopped)**
- **1 small onion (diced)**
- **1 green bell pepper (diced)**
- **2 celery stalks (diced)**
- **4 cloves garlic (minced)**
- **1 tbsp Cajun seasoning** (adjust to taste)
- **1 tsp smoked paprika**
- **1/2 tsp dried thyme**
- **1/2 tsp dried oregano**
- **1/2 tsp salt** (adjust as needed)
- **1/2 tsp black pepper**
- **1/2 tsp cayenne pepper** (optional for extra spice)
- **2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce**
- **2 green onions (chopped, for garnish)**
- **2 tbsp fresh parsley (chopped, for garnish)**

---

#### **Instructions:**

1. **Cook the Rice:**
    
    - In a pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil.
    - Add the rice, reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for **15 minutes** or until the rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
    - Remove from heat, fluff with a fork, and set aside.
2. **Brown the Meat:**
    
    - Heat **1 tbsp olive oil** in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
    - Add the ground beef (or pork sausage) and chicken livers (if using).
    - Cook, breaking up the meat with a spoon, until browned and fully cooked, about **8-10 minutes**.
    - Drain any excess fat if needed.
3. **Sauté the Vegetables:**
    
    - Add the diced onion, bell pepper, and celery (the Cajun “Holy Trinity”) to the skillet.
    - Cook for **5 minutes**, stirring occasionally, until softened.
    - Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another **30 seconds** until fragrant.
4. **Season the Dish:**
    
    - Sprinkle in the Cajun seasoning, smoked paprika, thyme, oregano, salt, black pepper, and cayenne (if using).
    - Stir well to combine and cook for **2 minutes**, allowing the flavors to meld.
5. **Combine Ingredients:**
    
    - Add the cooked rice to the skillet and stir everything together.
    - Pour in the Worcestershire sauce and mix thoroughly to ensure the rice is coated in the seasoning and meat mixture.
    - Cook for another **5 minutes**, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to develop.
6. **Finish and Serve:**
    
    - Remove from heat and garnish with chopped green onions and parsley.
    - Serve hot with your favorite Cajun or Creole dishes, such as grilled chicken, blackened fish, or fried catfish.

---

#### **Tips:**

- **Make it spicier** by adding more cayenne or a few dashes of hot sauce.
- **For an authentic touch,** use chicken livers, which add richness and depth of flavor.
- **Storage:** Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to **3 days** or freeze for up to **3 months.**
