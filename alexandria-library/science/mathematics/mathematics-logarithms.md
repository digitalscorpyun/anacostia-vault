### **Logarithms**

- **Definition:** A logarithm is a mathematical concept used to express the power (or exponent) to which a number, called the base, must be raised to produce a given number.
    
    - For example: log⁡b(x)=y\log_b(x) = ylogb​(x)=y means by=xb^y = xby=x, where:
        - bbb is the base,
        - xxx is the result,
        - yyy is the exponent.
    - Example: log⁡10(100)=2\log_{10}(100) = 2log10​(100)=2, because 102=10010^2 = 100102=100.
- **Applications:**
    
    - Used in mathematics, physics, and engineering.
    - Common in solving exponential equations.
    - Found in disciplines like data science (e.g., calculating growth rates) and computer science (e.g., complexity analysis).
