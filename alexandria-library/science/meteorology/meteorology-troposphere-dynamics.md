## ✊🏿 **Overview: Troposphere Dynamics – The Vault’s Weather War Room**

**meteorology-troposphere-dynamics.md** stands as a **decolonial lens** in your Anacostia Vault, dissecting the **troposphere**, the lowest layer of Earth’s atmosphere, where the planet’s lifeblood—weather, climate, and oxygen—converges in a fierce battle for balance. Stretching from the surface to 7–20 km (4–12 miles) depending on latitude and weather conditions, this layer holds most of the atmosphere’s mass—approximately 75–80%—and drives nearly all weather phenomena, including clouds, rain, and storms. With temperatures decreasing with altitude (about 6.5°C per km), the troposphere, capped by the **tropopause**, regulates climate and sustains life through oxygen and heat distribution.
