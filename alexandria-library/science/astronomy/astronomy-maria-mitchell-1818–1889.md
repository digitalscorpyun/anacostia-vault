## 📜 **The 👑 Royal Purple Vision – Maria Mitchell’s Stellar Legacy**

1. **Early Life and Education** 🟣  
   Born August 1, 1818, in Nantucket, Massachusetts, to Quaker parents William and Lydia Mitchell, Maria was the third of ten children. Her father, an amateur astronomer, nurtured her passion for the stars, teaching her to use telescopes and calculate celestial events—[[africana-studies-educational-equity]]. At 12, she assisted in timing a solar eclipse, showcasing her early brilliance—[[cultural-diversity-identity]].

2. **Comet Discovery** 🟣  
   On October 1, 1847, Mitchell discovered a telescopic comet (C/1847 T1), later named “Miss Mitchell’s Comet,” earning her a gold medal from King Christian VIII of Denmark in 1848—[[astronomy-comet-discoveries]]. This 🏰 royal purple achievement made her the first American woman recognized internationally as a professional astronomer—[[africana-studies-black-technology-innovators]].

3. **Vassar College and Research** 🟣  
   In 1865, Mitchell joined Vassar College as its first astronomy professor and observatory director, using a 12-inch telescope to study sunspots, Jupiter, and Saturn—[[astronomy-planetary-studies]]. She pioneered daily sunspot photography, revealing their nature as whirling cavities, not clouds, and led eclipse expeditions—[[social-justice]].

4. **Advocacy for Equity** 🟣  
   Mitchell fought for pay equity at Vassar, securing equal salary with male peers, and co-founded the Association for the Advancement of Women (AAW) in 1873, serving as president (1875–1876)—[[africana-studies-systemic-racism]]. Her 👑 royal purple defiance challenged gender barriers—[[the-lion-of-anacostia-bias-detection]].

5. **Decolonial Lens** 🟣
