### **Overview:**

Vera Rubin (1928–2016) 🌍 was an American astronomer whose groundbreaking work provided strong evidence for the existence of **dark matter** 🌑. Her research on galaxy rotation curves challenged existing theories and reshaped modern astrophysics. 🚀

---

### **Key Details:**

- **Born:** July 23, 1928, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania 🎂
- **Died:** December 25, 2016 ⚰️
- **Field:** Astronomy, Astrophysics 🌌
- **Known For:** Proving the existence of dark matter through the study of galaxy rotation speeds 🌠
- **Notable Work:** Studied the Andromeda Galaxy 🌌, revealing discrepancies that pointed to unseen mass (dark matter)
- **Education:** 🎓
    - B.A., Vassar College
    - M.A., Cornell University
    - Ph.D., Georgetown University

---

### **Timeline:** ⏳

- **1960s:** Began studying galaxy rotation curves 🔭
- **1970s:** Provided conclusive evidence of dark matter 🌑
- **1993:** Awarded the National Medal of Science 🏅
