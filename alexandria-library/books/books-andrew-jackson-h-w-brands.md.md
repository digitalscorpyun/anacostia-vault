## 🔑 Key Insights  
### 🏛️ Early Life  
- Andrew Jackson wasn’t what most of his neighbors would have called genteel, but by the end of the first decade of the nineteenth century his rough edges were beginning to rub smooth (Chapter 12) .  
- He was one of the best known men in Tennessee, an important figure in state politics (ibid).
- His home was the Hermitage, a property he acquired in 1804 (ibid). 
- By 1820 he held four dozen slaves, including his cook and house servants, by 1829 he held perhaps a hundred.
