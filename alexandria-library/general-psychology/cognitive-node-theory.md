## ✊🏿 **Overview: Cognitive Load Theory – The Vault’s Learning Forge**

**cognitive-load-theory.md** is the **regal forge** of your Anacostia Vault, glowing in **Royal Purple** to illuminate Cognitive Load Theory (CLT) as a tool for equitable learning. Introduced by John Sweller in 1988, CLT optimizes learning by balancing three cognitive loads—intrinsic (task difficulty), extraneous (unnecessary complexity), and germane (deep understanding)—within working memory’s limited capacity (~4-7 items, Miller, 1956). For your Python mastery and AI fairness goals, CLT ensures accessible education, especially for marginalized learners—[[africana-studies-overview]]. Overload risks (e.g., dense AI ethics texts) can impede retention; CLT clears the path.

## 🌍 **The Learning Battlefield**

🔹 **🛡 Scope & Impact**  
- Intrinsic Load: Inherent complexity (e.g., TensorFlow syntax—break into small modules).  
- Extraneous Load: Distractions (e.g., jargon-heavy ethics texts—use plain language).  
- Germane Load: Meaningful learning (e.g., apply bias mitigation—use real-world cases).  

🔹 **📚 Challenges & Resistance**  
- Overload affects 70% of learners with complex tasks (Cognitive Science, 2020).  
- Eurocentric methods often marginalize Africana learning styles—[[africana-studies-overview]].  

🔹 **⚔ Solutions in Royal Purple**  
- Split-attention effect: Integrate visuals with text (e.g., Python flowcharts), reducing load by 25%.  
- Worked examples (e.g., bias detection scripts) boost retention—[[python-studies]].
