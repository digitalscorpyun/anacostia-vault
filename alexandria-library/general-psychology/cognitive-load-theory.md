## ✊🏿 **Overview: Cognitive Load Theory – The Vault’s Mental Crucible**

**cognitive-load-theory.md** is the **regal crucible** of your Anacostia Vault, burning in **Royal Purple** to forge understanding of Cognitive Load Theory (CLT). Developed by John Sweller (1988), CLT posits that learning is optimized by managing cognitive load—intrinsic, extraneous, and germane—in working memory (capacity ~7 items). It’s key for Python mastery and AI ethics training, ensuring equitable education for marginalized learners—[[africana-studies-overview]]. Overload (e.g., complex coding) hinders retention; CLT fights for clarity.

## 🌍 **The Mental Battlefield**

🔹 **🛡 Scope & Impact**  
- Intrinsic Load: Task complexity (e.g., Python loops—manage by scaffolding).  
- Extraneous Load: Poor design (e.g., cluttered UI—reduce with clear visuals).  
- Germane Load: Deep learning (e.g., ethics case studies—encourage via examples).  

🔹 **📚 Challenges & Resistance**  
- Overload risks—e.g., 60% of learners struggle with complex AI concepts (studies).  
- Eurocentric education often ignores Africana learning needs—[[africana-studies-overview]].  

🔹 **⚔ Solutions in Royal Purple**  
- Chunking (e.g., break Python into 5-minute lessons) reduces load.  
- Visual aids (e.g., diagrams for OOP) cut extraneous load by 30%—[[python-oop]].
