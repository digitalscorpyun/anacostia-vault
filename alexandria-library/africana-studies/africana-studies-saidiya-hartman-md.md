# 🌟 Saidiya Hartman (b. 1961)  
**Key Themes**: Archival violence 🗃️⚡ | Afterlife of slavery ⛓️👻 | Speculative history 🌌 | Black feminist praxis ✊🏿📖  

---

## 📜 **Key Themes**  
1. **[[Afterlife of Slavery]]**: The enduring impact of slavery on Black life, where "**emancipation** is not the end of the story, but the beginning of new forms of subjection" (*Lose Your Mother*).  
2. **[[Critical Fabulation]]**: A method of "**speculative storytelling**" to recover erased Black voices in archives 🗃️➡️🎭.  
3. **[[Archival Silencing]]**: Critique of archives as sites of violence that erase Black suffering and resistance.  
4. **[[Black Girlhood]]**: Explores the precarity and resilience of Black girls in *Wayward Lives, Beautiful Experiments* 🌸⚡.  

---

## 🌍 **Historical Context**  
- Hartman’s work emerges from **post-Civil Rights era scholarship**, bridging Black Studies, feminism, and critical theory.  
- Responds to the **limitations of traditional historiography** in capturing Black lived experience, especially under slavery and Jim Crow.  
- Influenced by **Black feminist thought** (Hortense Spillers, Audre Lorde) and **Afro-pessimism** (Frank Wilderson III).  

---

## ✨ **Philosophical Insights**  
- **"The Terrible Beauty of the Chorus"** (*Scenes of Subjection*): How enslaved people transformed rituals of violence (e.g., forced singing) into acts of survival and subversion 🎶⚡.  
- **Ethics of Representation**: "How does one **tell the story** of an encounter with the archive when the archive is **founded on violence**?" (*Venus in Two Acts*).  
- **Radical Care**: Reimagines Black intimacy and community in *Wayward Lives* as acts of resistance to carceral systems 🏚️➡️💖.  

---

## 🔗 **Connections to Africana Thought**  
- [[Cedric Robinson]]: Both critique **racial capitalism** and the dehumanizing logics of modernity.  
- [[Michel-Rolph Trouillot]]: Shared focus on **silencing in historical narratives** (*Silencing the Past*).  
- [[Christina Sharpe]]: Hartman’s "afterlife" concept parallels Sharpe’s "**wake**" as a space of ongoing Black survival (*In the Wake*).  
- [[Toni Morrison]]: Literary kinship in centering Black interiority and haunting legacies (*Beloved*).  

---

## 🛠️ **Practical Applications**  
- **Decolonizing Archives**: Inspire projects like *[[Freedom on the Move]]* (database of runaway slave ads) or *[[Enslaved.org]]*.  
- **Social Justice Advocacy**: Frame modern policing 🚔 and mass incarceration as **afterlives of slavery** (cf. [[Michelle Alexander]]).  
- **Artistic Collaboration**: Hartman’s work influences filmmakers ([[Arthur Jafa]]) and poets ([[Claudia Rankine]]).  

---

## 📖 **Major Works**  
1. *[[Scenes of Subjection]]* (1997) 🎭⚡: Examines slavery’s "terror and enjoyment" in everyday life.  
2. *[[Lose Your Mother]]* (2007) 🌍💔: A memoir-theory hybrid tracing the slave trade’s routes in Ghana.  
3. *[[Wayward Lives, Beautiful Experiments]]* (2019) 🌸✨: Reimagines Black queer and feminist rebellion in early 20th-century cities.  

---

## 🔍 **Suggested Further Reading**  
- [[Hortense Spillers]]’ *Mama’s Baby, Papa’s Maybe*: On gendered racial violence.

- [[Achille Mbembe]]’s *Necropolitics*: Theorizes Black expendability.  
- [[Kara Walker]]’s art: Visualizes Hartman’s themes of violence and memory.
