## Overview
**Martin Luther King Jr.** (1929–1968) was a Baptist minister, activist, and one of the most prominent leaders of the **American Civil Rights Movement**. He championed nonviolent resistance to racial injustice and played a crucial role in ending legalized segregation and discrimination in the United States. King's philosophy was heavily influenced by Mahatma Gandhi’s principles of nonviolence and Christian teachings on love and justice.

## Key Contributions

### **1. Civil Rights Leadership**
- Led the **Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955–1956)**, a pivotal moment in the fight against segregation.  
- Advocated for **nonviolent protest**, organizing peaceful demonstrations that challenged systemic racism.  

### **2. March on Washington (1963)**
- Organized the event that attracted over **250,000 people**, where he delivered his famous _“I Have a Dream”_ speech advocating for racial and economic justice.  

### **3. Legislative Achievements**
- Played an instrumental role in influencing the passage of:  
    - **Civil Rights Act of 1964**, ending segregation in public places.  
    - **Voting Rights Act of 1965**, ensuring equal voting rights for African Americans.  

---

## Philosophy and Approach
- Inspired by Gandhi’s principle of **nonviolent resistance (Satyagraha)** and Christian values of love and justice.  
- Advocated for peaceful protests, sit-ins, and civil disobedience as effective strategies against oppression.  

---

## Impact & Legacy

1. **Legacy in Civil Rights:**  
    - King’s work directly contributed to the dismantling of Jim Crow laws and the expansion of civil liberties for African Americans.  
    - His speeches and writings continue to inspire global movements for justice and equality.  

2. **Recognition and Honors:**  
    - Posthumously awarded the **Presidential Medal of Freedom** and **Congressional Gold Medal**.  
    - Martin Luther King Jr. Day was established as a U.S. federal holiday in **1986**.  

3. **Cultural Impact:**  
    - His speeches and leadership influenced social justice movements worldwide.  
    - Institutions and streets named in his honor globally.

## Criticism and Challenges
- Faced opposition from segregationists, law enforcement agencies, and even some within the civil rights movement who considered his nonviolent approach too passive.  
- The FBI surveilled King extensively under the COINTELPRO program, branding him a potential threat.
