# The Crisis

**Type:** Publication  
**Date Created:** 2025-01-26  
**Last Updated:** 2025-01-26  

## Overview

**_The Crisis_** is the official magazine of the **National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP)**. Founded in **1910** by civil rights activist and scholar **[[W.E.B. Du Bois]]**, it is the oldest Black-oriented magazine in the United States. The publication serves as a powerful platform for addressing issues of racial justice, civil rights, and African-American culture.

---

## Key Details

- **Founder:** [[W.E.B. Du Bois]]  
- **First Issue:** November 1910  
- **Publisher:** [[NAACP]]  
- **Mission:** To educate and advocate for African-American social, political, and economic rights.  
- **Publication Frequency:** Quarterly (originally monthly)  
- **Current Status:** Continues to be published in print and digital formats.  

---

## Timeline

- **1910 (20th Century):** _The Crisis_ is founded by W.E.B. Du Bois to support the civil rights efforts of the NAACP.  
- **1919 (20th Century):** The magazine plays a critical role in documenting the **Red Summer** race riots and lynchings.  
- **1920s (20th Century):** _The Crisis_ becomes a key outlet during the **Harlem Renaissance**, publishing work by emerging Black writers and intellectuals.  
- **1950s–1960s (20th Century):** Focuses on civil rights struggles, including the Montgomery Bus Boycott and the March on Washington.  
- **Present Day (21st Century):** Continues to provide commentary on modern civil rights issues and African-American culture.

## Editorial Focus

1. **Civil Rights and Advocacy**  
   - Addresses racial inequality in education, housing, voting rights, and employment.  
   - Reports on landmark legal cases, protests, and advocacy campaigns.  

2. **African-American Culture and Achievements**  
   - Celebrates contributions of Black leaders in literature, music, sports, and business.  
   - Highlights African-American success stories and cultural milestones.  

3. **Political Commentary**  
   - Provides analysis of U.S. policies affecting African Americans.  
   - Advocates for legislative changes to combat systemic racism.  

4. **Literature and the Arts**  
   - Published works from key figures such as:  
     - **[[Langston Hughes]]**  
     - **[[Zora Neale Hurston]]**  
     - **[[Claude McKay]]**  
     - **[[Jessie Redmon Fauset]]**, one of the magazine's notable editors.  

---

## Impact and Legacy

1. **Platform for Black Intellectuals**  
   - _The Crisis_ provided an essential platform for Black scholars, writers, and activists to express their views at a time when mainstream media ignored African-American voices.  

2. **Role in the Harlem Renaissance**  
   - The magazine was instrumental in promoting and preserving the literary and artistic contributions of African Americans during the Harlem Renaissance.  

3. **Influence on the Civil Rights Movement**  
   - Served as a key publication for civil rights leaders and activists in the 1950s and 60s.  

4. **Modern Influence**  
   - Continues to engage contemporary readers by addressing issues like police brutality, economic inequality, and Black representation in media.

## Notable Editors

- **W.E.B. Du Bois (1910–1934):** Founding editor and key voice in shaping the magazine's advocacy focus.  
- **Roy Wilkins:** Oversaw the magazine during the height of the Civil Rights Movement.  
- **Jesse Redmon Fauset:** Literary editor who helped spotlight Black writers and intellectuals.
