## 📜 **The 👑 Royal Purple Legacy – William Washington Browne’s Journey**

1. **Early Life and Escape** 🟣  
   Born Ben Browne on October 20, 1849, in Habersham County, Georgia, to enslaved parents Joseph and Mariah Browne, he was sold at age eight to a horse trader near Memphis, adopting the name William Washington—[[africana-studies-african-origins]]. He escaped slavery in 1862 after Union forces occupied Memphis, serving on a Union gunboat and in the infantry—[[cultural-diversity-identity]].

2. **Education and Ministry** 🟣  
   Discharged in 1866, Browne attended school in Prairie du Chien, Wisconsin, then taught in the South from 1869, marrying Mary A. Graham in 1873—[[africana-studies-educational-equity]]. Ordained a Methodist minister in 1876, he spoke against the Ku Klux Klan and advocated temperance—[[africana-studies-systemic-racism]].

3. **True Reformers and Bank** 🟣  
   In 1881, Browne transformed the True Reformers in Richmond into a fraternal order, chartering the True Reformers Savings Bank in 1888—the first Black-owned bank in the U.S.—[[africana-studies-black-technology-innovators]]. It opened in 1889 at his home (105 West Jackson Street), peaking at over $1 million in deposits by 1907—[[social-justice]].
