## Overview
**Charlotta Bass** (1874–1969) was a pioneering African-American journalist, civil rights activist, and politician. She is best known for her leadership of the _California Eagle_, one of the most influential Black newspapers in the United States, and for becoming the first African-American woman to run for vice president of the United States.

---

## Key Details

- **Full Name:** Charlotta Amanda Spears Bass  
- **Born:** February 14, 1874, in Sumter, South Carolina  
- **Died:** April 12, 1969, in Los Angeles, California  
- **Occupation:** Journalist, Civil Rights Activist, Politician  
- **Known For:**  
  - Ownership of the _California Eagle_  
  - Advocacy for civil rights and social justice  
  - First African-American woman vice-presidential candidate (1952)  

---

## Timeline

- **1910 (20th Century):** Moved to Los Angeles for health reasons.  
- **1912 (20th Century):** Took over ownership of the _California Eagle_ after the death of John Neimore.  
- **1914 (20th Century):** Married Joseph Bass, who co-managed the paper with her.  
- **1951 (20th Century):** Retired from the _California Eagle_ and continued activism.  
- **1952 (20th Century):** Ran for vice president under the Progressive Party ticket.  
- **1969 (20th Century):** Passed away in Los Angeles, California.

## Journalism Career

### **1. Leadership of the _California Eagle_**
- Became editor and publisher, transforming the paper into a powerful tool for racial and social justice.  
- Exposed police brutality, employment discrimination, and housing segregation.  
- Fought against the Ku Klux Klan's influence in California and beyond.  

### **2. Advocacy Through Journalism**
- Promoted civil rights, fair housing, labor rights, and educational equality.  
- Encouraged African-American political participation and voter registration.  
- Championed the rights of immigrants and other marginalized groups.  

---

## Civil Rights Activism

Charlotta Bass fought tirelessly for:

1. **Fair Housing:**  
   - Opposed restrictive housing covenants that denied African Americans homeownership rights.  

2. **Education:**  
   - Advocated for equal access to quality education for Black children.  

3. **Voting Rights:**  
   - Launched campaigns to encourage Black voter participation and political engagement.  

4. **Labor Rights:**  
   - Supported unionization efforts and fought for equal wages for African-American workers.  

**Opposition to:**  
- Lynching and mob violence.  
- Jim Crow segregation laws.  

---

## Political Career

### **Vice Presidential Run (1952)**
- Ran for vice president on the **Progressive Party** ticket alongside presidential candidate **Vincent Hallinan**.  
- Focused on issues such as:  
  - Civil rights  
  - Labor rights  
  - Anti-war policies  
  - Social justice  

**Campaign Slogan:**  
_"Win or lose, we win by raising the issues."_  

- Faced harassment and FBI surveillance due to her activism and progressive policies.  

---

## Legacy and Impact

1. **Trailblazer in Journalism and Politics:**  
   - First African-American woman to own and operate a major newspaper.  
   - Inspired future generations of Black women in media and politics.  

2. **Social Justice Influence:**  
   - Instrumental in advancing African-American rights through both journalism and activism.  
   - Continues to serve as a role model for journalists and civil rights activists.  

3. **Cultural Recognition:**  
   - Honored in museums, exhibitions, and educational materials for her groundbreaking contributions.  

**Africana Studies Relation:**  
Charlotta Bass' work provides critical insights into the role of Black media in social justice movements and remains a foundational figure in the study of African-American political activism and journalism.
