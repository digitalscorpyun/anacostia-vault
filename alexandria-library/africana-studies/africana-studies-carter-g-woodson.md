# Africana Studies - Carter G. Woodson 📜🎓

**Type:** Historical Figure  
 

---

## Overview 🏛️  
**Carter G. Woodson** (1875–1950) was a historian, educator, and author known as the **"Father of Black History."** He founded the **Association for the Study of Negro Life and History (ASNLH)** in 1915 and launched **Negro History Week** in 1926, which later became **Black History Month** in 1976.

---

## Key Details 📝  
- **Full Name:** Carter Godwin Woodson  
- **Birthdate:** December 19, 1875  
- **Birthplace:** New Canton, Virginia, USA  
- **Education:**  
  - **B.A.**, Berea College (1903) 🎓  
  - **M.A.**, University of Chicago (1908)  
  - **Ph.D.**, Harvard University (1912) (Second Black person after W.E.B. Du Bois to earn a Ph.D. from Harvard)  
- **Major Contributions:**  
  - Founded the **ASNLH** (*now ASALH* - Association for the Study of African American Life and History).  
  - Created **Negro History Week** (1926), later expanded into **Black History Month**.  
  - Wrote *The Mis-Education of the Negro* (1933), a landmark book critiquing the U.S. education system’s neglect of Black history.  
  - Launched *The Journal of Negro History* in 1916 to promote academic research on Black history.  
- **Death:** April 3, 1950  
- [Biography](https://encyclopediavirginia.org/entries/woodson-carter-g-1875-1950/)

## Timeline 📅  
- **1875:** Born in New Canton, Virginia.  
- **1895:** Worked in coal mines to support his education.  
- **1897:** Enrolled in Douglass High School, completing four years in just two years.  
- **1903:** Earned **Bachelor’s degree** from Berea College.  
- **1908:** Earned **Master’s degree** from the University of Chicago.  
- **1912:** Earned **Ph.D. from Harvard**, becoming the **second African American to do so**.  
- **1915:** Founded the **ASNLH** to preserve Black history.  
- **1916:** Established *The Journal of Negro History*.  
- **1926:** Created **Negro History Week** (precursor to Black History Month).  
- **1933:** Published *The Mis-Education of the Negro*.  
- **1941:** Launched the first **federally funded Black history project**.  
- **1950:** Passed away in Washington, D.C.  
- **1976:** Negro History Week expanded into **Black History Month**.

## Impact & Legacy 🌍  
Carter G. Woodson revolutionized the study of **Black history** by ensuring it was properly documented and recognized. His work **challenged the racial biases of mainstream historical narratives** and laid the foundation for future generations of scholars, educators, and activists.

**Africana Studies Relation:**  
Woodson’s contributions were crucial in shaping the **Black intellectual tradition**, establishing **historical consciousness**, and promoting the inclusion of African American history in mainstream education.
