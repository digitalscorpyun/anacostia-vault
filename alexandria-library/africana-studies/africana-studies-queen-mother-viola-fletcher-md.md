## ✨ **Overview**

Queen Mother Viola Ford Fletcher, born May 10, 1914, is the oldest known living survivor of the 1921 Tulsa Race Massacre. A supercentenarian and global symbol of resilience, she has dedicated her life to advocating for justice, reparations, and racial equity. Crowned as a Queen Mother in Ghana, Fletcher’s legacy transcends borders, representing the enduring fight against systemic racism and the reclamation of Black history and identity.

---

## 📌 **Key Details**

- **Full Name:** Viola Ford Fletcher
- **Born:** May 10, 1914, Comanche, Oklahoma 🇺🇸
- **Affectionately Known As:** "Mother Fletcher" 🤍
- **Title:** Queen Mother (Ghana, 2021) 👑
- **Occupation:** Advocate for Justice & Racial Equity ✊🏾
- **Notable Roles:**
    - Survivor of the Tulsa Race Massacre (1921) 🔥
    - Founder of the Viola Ford Fletcher Foundation 🌍
    - Author of _Don’t Let Them Bury My Story_ 📚
- **Family:** Daughter of Lucinda Ellis and John Wesley Ford, sister to Hughes Van Ellis

---

## 🗓️ **Timeline**

- **1914:** Born in Comanche, Oklahoma
- **1921:** Survived the Tulsa Race Massacre at age 7 🏚️
- **1932:** Married Robert Fletcher 💍
- **2021:** Testified before U.S. Congress on the Tulsa Race Massacre ✊🏾
- **2021:** Crowned Queen Mother in Ghana 🇬🇭
- **2023:** Published memoir _Don’t Let Them Bury My Story_ 📖
- **2024:** Celebrated 110th birthday, becoming a supercentenarian 🎉

## 🌍 **Impact & Legacy**

Queen Mother Fletcher’s life embodies resilience, survival, and the fight for justice. Through her testimony before Congress, she has amplified the voices of Tulsa Race Massacre survivors, demanding reparations and recognition. Her foundation promotes racial equity, community empowerment, and the preservation of Black history.

---

## ✊🏾 **Africana Studies Relation**

Fletcher’s story is deeply rooted in the global African diaspora narrative. From the trauma of Greenwood’s destruction to her recognition in Ghana, she bridges African American history with Pan-African solidarity. Her journey highlights the interconnected struggles against colonialism, systemic racism, and the fight for restorative justice across the diaspora.
