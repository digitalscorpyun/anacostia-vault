## 📜 **The 👑 Royal Purple Visionary – Scipio Moorhead’s Story**

1. **Early Life and Enslavement** 🟣  
   Born c. 1750–1760 in Africa, Moorhead was enslaved by Reverend John Moorhead in Boston. His talents were nurtured by Sarah Moorhead, an art teacher—[[africana-studies-educational-equity]]. This 👑 royal purple beginning defies erasure.

2. **Artistic Emergence** 🟣  
   Active c. 1773, Moorhead may have created Wheatley’s frontispiece for *Poems on Various Subjects, Religious and Moral* (1773)—[[phillis-wheatley-portrait]]. This 🏰 royal purple work, possibly the first portrait of a Black woman writing, challenges racist narratives.

3. **Recognition and Uncertainty** 🟣  
   Wheatley’s poem “To S. M. a Young African Painter” honors him, but no signed works survive. Scholars like William H. Robinson (1984) suggest his genius—[[africana-studies-oral-traditions]]. This ✨ royal purple ambiguity critiques historical neglect.
