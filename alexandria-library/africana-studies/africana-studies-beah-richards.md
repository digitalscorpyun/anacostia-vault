# Beah Richards (1920–2000)

**Type:** Person  
**Date Created:** 2025-01-26

## Overview
**Beah Richards** (July 12, 1920 – September 14, 2000) was an acclaimed African American actress, poet, and playwright known for her powerful performances on stage and screen, as well as her activism in the fight for racial and social justice. Her career spanned several decades, during which she became a respected figure in both the entertainment industry and civil rights movements.

---

## Key Details

- **Full Name:** Beulah Elizabeth Richardson  
- **Born:** July 12, 1920, Vicksburg, Mississippi  
- **Died:** September 14, 2000, Vicksburg, Mississippi  
- **Occupation:** Actress, Playwright, Poet, Civil Rights Advocate  
- **Known For:**  
  - _Guess Who's Coming to Dinner_ (1967)  
  - _A Black Woman Speaks_ (1950)  
  - Advocacy for racial and gender justice

## Key Achievements and Contributions

### **1. Acting Career**
- Gained widespread recognition for her role in _Guess Who's Coming to Dinner_ (1967), earning an **Academy Award nomination** for Best Supporting Actress.  
- Starred in iconic plays such as **_The Amen Corner_** and **_A Raisin in the Sun_**, showcasing her commanding stage presence.  
- Received **multiple Emmy Awards**, including for roles in _The Practice_ and _Frank's Place_.  

### **2. Playwriting and Poetry**
- Authored _A Black Woman Speaks_ (1950), a powerful critique of racism and sexism, advocating for Black empowerment and women's rights.  
- Her literary works explored themes of race, gender, and social justice, resonating with generations of activists and artists.  

### **3. Civil Rights Advocacy**
- Collaborated with prominent activists such as **Paul Robeson** and **Harry Belafonte** in the fight for racial equality.  
- Used her public platform to raise awareness about systemic oppression and the importance of self-determination for African Americans.  
- Advocated for the inclusion of Black narratives in mainstream theater and film.

## Challenges and Legacy

### **1. Overcoming Racial Barriers**
- Confronted the challenges of racial typecasting and limited opportunities for Black actors in Hollywood.  
- Paved the way for future Black actresses by refusing to conform to stereotypical roles and demanding substantive, dignified portrayals.  

### **2. Cultural Impact**
- Richards' work continues to inspire discussions on representation, race, and gender in American arts and culture.

### **3. Posthumous Recognition**
