# 🏛 **Africana Studies - Frantz Fanon**

> _“Each generation must, out of relative obscurity, discover its mission, fulfill it, or betray it.”_ — Frantz Fanon

---

## 📌 **Overview**

**Frantz Fanon** (July 20, 1925 – December 6, 1961) was a **revolutionary psychiatrist, philosopher, political theorist, and anti-colonial activist**. Born in **Martinique**, he became one of the most influential voices on **decolonization, race, and liberation movements**. His works, particularly _[[Africana Studies - Black Skin, White Masks]]_ and _[[Africana Studies - The Wretched of the Earth]]_, analyze the psychological effects of colonialism and offer a roadmap for revolutionary resistance.

Fanon’s ideas inspired **African independence movements, the Black Power movement, and contemporary struggles against systemic racism and imperialism**.

---

## 🏷️ **Key Details**

- **Full Name:** Frantz Omar Fanon
- **Born:** July 20, 1925 – Fort-de-France, Martinique
- **Died:** December 6, 1961 – Bethesda, Maryland, USA
- **Education:**
    - Studied medicine and psychiatry at the **University of Lyon, France**
- **Occupations:** Psychiatrist, Writer, Revolutionary, Political Theorist
- **Major Works:**
    - _[[Africana Studies - Black Skin, White Masks]]_ (1952)
    - _[[Africana Studies - The Wretched of the Earth]]_ (1961)
    - _A Dying Colonialism_ (1959)
    - _Toward the African Revolution_ (1964, posthumous)

## 📜 **Timeline**

📍 **1925** – Born in French-ruled Martinique, experiences racial discrimination under colonial rule.  
📍 **1944** – Joins the **Free French Forces** to fight against Nazi occupation in WWII.  
📍 **1947-1952** – Studies psychiatry and philosophy in France, publishes _Black Skin, White Masks_.  
📍 **1953** – Begins working at a psychiatric hospital in Algeria, witnessing **colonial violence firsthand**.  
📍 **1956** – Resigns from his position, joins the **Algerian National Liberation Front (FLN)** in their fight for independence.  
📍 **1961** – Publishes _The Wretched of the Earth_, a revolutionary call for armed resistance against colonialism.  
📍 **1961** – Dies from leukemia in the U.S. at age 36.

## 🔥 **Key Ideas & Philosophies**

### **1️⃣ The Psychological Impact of Colonialism**

- Described how **racism warps Black identity** and creates **self-hatred among the colonized**.
- Compared colonial rule to **mental illness**, requiring **revolution for true healing**.

🔗 **Related Notes:**

- [[Africana Studies - Internalized Racism & Self-Perception]]
- [[Africana Studies - The Cultural Erasure of Colonized Peoples]]

---

### **2️⃣ Revolutionary Violence as Decolonization**

- Stated that **the oppressed must reclaim their humanity through active resistance**.
- Argued that colonial powers **only understand force**, making armed revolution necessary.

- [[Africana Studies - The Role of Violence in Liberation Struggles]]

### **3️⃣ The Pitfalls of Post-Colonial Leadership**

- Warned against **neocolonial elites** who replaced European rulers but maintained **colonial power structures**.
- Called for **true socialism and economic self-reliance** in newly independent African nations.

## ⚖️ **Challenges & Opposition**

⚠️ **Banned & Censored** – His books were banned in **France, South Africa, and former colonies**.  
⚠️ **Criticism from Nonviolent Activists** – His advocacy for **armed resistance** was controversial.  
⚠️ **Overlooked in Western Philosophy** – His works were marginalized in academia until the rise of **postcolonial studies**.
