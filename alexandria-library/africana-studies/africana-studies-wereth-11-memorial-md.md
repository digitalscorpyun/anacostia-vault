# Wereth 11 Memorial

**Type:** Memorial  
**Date Created:** 2025-01-26  
**Last Updated:** 2025-01-26  

## Overview  
The **Wereth 11 Memorial** is a site dedicated to honoring the memory of **11 African American soldiers** of the **333rd Field Artillery Battalion** who were brutally murdered by Nazi SS troops during the **Battle of the Bulge** in **World War II**. The memorial, located in **Wereth, Belgium**, serves as a tribute to their courage and a reminder of the racial injustices faced by Black soldiers during the war.

---

## Key Details  

- **Location:** Wereth, Belgium  
- **Dedicated to:** The 11 African American soldiers of the 333rd Field Artillery Battalion  
- **Incident Date:** December 17, 1944  
- **Established:** 1994 by Hermann Langer, a local Belgian citizen  

### **The Wereth 11 Soldiers**  
The soldiers honored at the memorial are:  

1. Curtis Adams  
2. Mager Bradley  
3. George Davis  
4. Thomas Forte  
5. Robert Green  
6. James Leatherwood  
7. Nathaniel Moss  
8. George Moten  
9. William Pritchett  
10. James Stewart  
11. Due Turner  

---

## Historical Background  

- During the **Battle of the Bulge**, these soldiers, part of the 333rd Field Artillery Battalion, were separated from their unit during the German offensive.  
- Seeking refuge, they surrendered to German forces after being given shelter by a local Belgian farmer, **Mathias Langer**, who risked his life to help them.  
- They were later captured, tortured, and executed by Nazi SS troops.  
- The atrocity was largely overlooked in official military reports, and their sacrifice remained unrecognized for decades.

---

## Recognition and Memorial Efforts  

- The **Wereth 11 Memorial** was established in **1994** by **Hermann Langer**, the son of the farmer who sheltered the soldiers.  
- It is the only European memorial specifically dedicated to African American soldiers who fought in World War II.  
- The site has become an important place of remembrance for African American contributions to the war effort and the racial injustices they endured.  
- In 2017, the U.S. Congress formally recognized the Wereth 11 and their sacrifice.

---

## Impact & Legacy  

The **Wereth 11 Memorial** stands as a poignant reminder of:  

1. **African American Contributions to WWII:** Despite facing discrimination within the military and society, Black soldiers served with distinction and bravery.  
2. **Recognition of Racial Injustice:** The massacre of the Wereth 11 highlights the racial inequalities African American soldiers endured even while fighting for their country.  
3. **Continued Advocacy:** The memorial has inspired ongoing efforts to ensure Black soldiers' contributions to WWII are properly acknowledged in history books and public memory.  

**Africana Studies Relation:**  
The Wereth 11 story intersects with Africana Studies by addressing themes of racial discrimination, military service, and historical erasure. It underscores the systemic challenges faced by Black soldiers in wartime and their resilience in the face of adversity.
