# Marcus Garvey

**Type:** Person  
**Date Created:** 2025-01-26  
**Last Updated:** 2025-01-26  

## Overview
**Marcus Garvey** (August 17, 1887 – June 10, 1940) was a Jamaican-born political leader, entrepreneur, and orator who founded the **Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA)** and advocated for Black nationalism, self-reliance, and Pan-Africanism. He remains one of the most influential figures in the global Black liberation movement, inspiring future generations with his message of African pride, unity, and economic independence.

---

## Key Details

- **Full Name:** Marcus Mosiah Garvey Jr.  
- **Born:** August 17, 1887, St. Ann's Bay, Jamaica  
- **Died:** June 10, 1940, London, England  
- **Occupation:** Political leader, journalist, entrepreneur, orator  
- **Known For:**  
  - Founding the **Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA)**  
  - Promoting the "Back to Africa" movement  
  - Establishing the **Black Star Line**, a shipping company aimed at economic empowerment  
  - Advocacy for Pan-Africanism and Black self-determination  

---

## Timeline

- **1914 (20th Century):** Founded the Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA) in Jamaica.  
- **1916 (20th Century):** Moved to the United States and expanded the UNIA in Harlem, New York.  
- **1919 (20th Century):** Launched the Black Star Line to facilitate economic independence for Africans and the African diaspora.  
- **1920 (20th Century):** Held the UNIA’s first international convention, drawing thousands to Harlem to promote Black pride and unity.  
- **1923 (20th Century):** Convicted of mail fraud in connection with the Black Star Line; later deported from the U.S.  
- **1940 (20th Century):** Passed away in London, largely in obscurity, but his ideas lived on in subsequent movements like the Civil Rights Movement and Pan-Africanism.

## Key Contributions and Achievements

### **1. Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA)**
- Established with the goal of promoting unity, economic independence, and pride among people of African descent worldwide.  
- At its peak, the UNIA had millions of members in over 40 countries.  
- Advocated for self-reliance, economic development, and the establishment of an independent African nation.

### **2. The Black Star Line**
- Founded as a steamship company aimed at facilitating trade and travel between Africa and the African diaspora.  
- Intended to enable Black economic autonomy and a return to Africa, but mismanagement and legal issues hindered its success.  

### **3. Back to Africa Movement**
- Promoted the idea that African Americans and others of African descent should return to Africa to build a new, self-sufficient nation.  
- Inspired African leaders and movements such as Ghana's independence under **Kwame Nkrumah**.

### **4. Influence on Future Movements**
- Garvey’s emphasis on Black pride and economic independence influenced figures such as **Malcolm X**, the Nation of Islam, and Pan-African leaders.  
- His philosophy of self-reliance became a cornerstone for Black empowerment movements.

---

## Philosophy and Ideology

- **Black Nationalism:** Advocated for economic and political independence for Black people globally.  
- **Self-Reliance:** Stressed the importance of Black-owned businesses and institutions to foster community strength.  
- **Pan-Africanism:** Supported global solidarity among African-descended people and their return to Africa.  
- **Racial Pride:** Encouraged Black people to embrace their heritage and reject European standards of success.  

---

## Challenges and Criticism

- **Conflict with W.E.B. Du Bois:** Du Bois and other leaders of the NAACP criticized Garvey’s methods and separatist ideals.  
- **Legal Troubles:** His conviction for mail fraud related to the Black Star Line weakened his influence and led to his deportation.  
- **Financial Difficulties:** Mismanagement and sabotage accusations led to the downfall of many of his business ventures.  

---

## Legacy and Impact

1. **Cultural Impact:**  
   - His ideas of Black pride and self-determination remain central themes in the African diaspora.  
   - "Garveyism" influenced the Rastafari movement in Jamaica, which considers him a prophet.  

2. **Political Influence:**  
   - Leaders such as **Kwame Nkrumah** and **Jomo Kenyatta** in Africa cited Garvey as an inspiration for decolonization.  
   - Civil Rights leaders in the U.S. incorporated aspects of his philosophy in their fight for equality.  

3. **Institutional Recognition:**  
   - Honored posthumously with monuments, street names, and recognition in Black history education.  

---

## Africana Studies Relation

Marcus Garvey’s teachings remain foundational in Africana Studies, offering insights into Black nationalism, economic empowerment, and Pan-Africanism. His work serves as a critical case study in analyzing strategies for racial uplift and community organization.
