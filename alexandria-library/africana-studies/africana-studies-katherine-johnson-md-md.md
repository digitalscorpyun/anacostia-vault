# Africana Studies - Katherine Johnson

> *"I like to learn. That's an art and a science."* — Katherine Johnson  

## 📝 Overview  
Katherine Johnson (1918–2020) was a **trailblazing African American mathematician** whose calculations were **critical to NASA’s early space missions**. She broke racial and gender barriers, contributing to the **Mercury, Apollo, and Space Shuttle programs**.  

Her work, along with that of fellow Black women mathematicians, was spotlighted in *Hidden Figures* (2016).  

---

## 📜 Early Life & Education  
- **Born:** August 26, 1918, in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia.  
- **Education:**  
  - **West Virginia State College** (Graduated at 18).  
  - One of the first Black women to integrate **West Virginia University’s** graduate math program (1939).  
- **Passion for Mathematics:** Displayed exceptional ability in **geometry and calculations** from a young age.  

---

## 🚀 NASA Career & Contributions  
### **West Area Computing Unit (1953–1958)**  
- Hired at **NACA (precursor to NASA)** as a "computer" in the segregated **West Area Computing unit** at Langley Research Center.  
- Quickly stood out for her **accuracy and analytical thinking**.  

### **Key NASA Contributions**  
| Mission | Role & Impact |
|---------|--------------|
| **Mercury-Atlas 6 (1962)** | Verified orbital calculations for **John Glenn**, who refused to fly until she manually confirmed them. |
| **Apollo 11 (1969)** | Provided trajectory analysis for the **first Moon landing**. |
| **Apollo 13 (1970)** | Assisted in recalculating return paths after the mission was damaged. |
| **Space Shuttle Program (1980s–1990s)** | Continued contributions in aeronautics and space flight research. |

---

## ⚖️ Breaking Barriers  
- One of the **first African American women** in an elite NASA research position.  
- Defied **segregation laws** and workplace discrimination, asserting herself in rooms dominated by white male engineers.  
- Advocated for **women and minorities in STEM**.  

---

## 🌍 Legacy & Recognition  
- **2015:** Awarded the **Presidential Medal of Freedom** by **President Barack Obama**.  
- **2016:** Portrayed by **Taraji P. Henson** in *Hidden Figures*.  
- **2017:** NASA’s **Langley Research Center** named a facility after her.  
- **2021:** Inducted into the **National Women’s Hall of Fame**.
