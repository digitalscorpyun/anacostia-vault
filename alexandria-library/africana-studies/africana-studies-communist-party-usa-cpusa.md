## Overview
The **Communist Party USA (CPUSA)** is a political organization in the United States that was founded in **1919**. Influenced by Marxist-Leninist principles, the CPUSA has been involved in various labor, civil rights, and social justice movements throughout its history. The party has historically advocated for workers' rights, racial and economic equality, and opposition to capitalist exploitation.

---

## Key Details

- **Founded:** 1919  
- **Founders:** Charles Ruthenberg, Louis Fraina, John Reed  
- **Headquarters:** New York City, NY  
- **Ideology:** Marxism-Leninism, socialism, anti-capitalism  
- **Major Publications:** _The Daily Worker_, _People's World_  

---

## Timeline

- **1919 (20th Century):** CPUSA founded in response to the Russian Revolution and the rise of global socialist movements.  
- **1930s (20th Century):** Actively supported labor movements during the Great Depression and fought for workers' rights through unions.  
- **1940s (20th Century):** Played a key role in the fight against fascism during World War II, aligning with the U.S. war effort after the Nazi invasion of the Soviet Union.  
- **1950s (20th Century):** Targeted by the U.S. government during the Red Scare, with members facing surveillance, arrests, and blacklisting.  
- **1960s (20th Century):** Supported the **Civil Rights Movement**, aligning with activists like Paul Robeson, W.E.B. Du Bois, and Angela Davis.  
- **1991 (20th Century):** Decline in influence following the collapse of the Soviet Union.  
- **21st Century:** Focuses on progressive social and economic policies, including healthcare, education, and racial justice.

## CPUSA and the African-American Community

Throughout its history, the CPUSA actively engaged with the African-American community, fighting for racial equality and economic justice. 

1. **Support for Civil Rights:**  
    - Advocated for anti-lynching laws and racial desegregation.  
    - Provided legal defense for falsely accused Black Americans, such as in the **Scottsboro Boys case**.  
    - Organized protests and labor strikes against racial discrimination in workplaces.

2. **Notable African-American Members:**  
    - **Paul Robeson:** Singer, actor, and political activist who was deeply involved in CPUSA’s fight for racial justice.  
    - **W.E.B. Du Bois:** Influential intellectual who embraced socialist ideals later in life and joined CPUSA in 1961.  
    - **Angela Davis:** Radical feminist and civil rights activist associated with CPUSA in the 1970s and 1980s.

3. **Black Belt Thesis:**  
    - A controversial proposal by CPUSA in the 1930s asserting that African Americans in the Southern U.S. constituted an oppressed nation with the right to self-determination.

---

## Key Issues and Policies

1. **Labor Rights:**  
    - Advocated for workers’ rights, fair wages, and unionization, often collaborating with organizations such as the **Brotherhood of Sleeping Car Porters**.  

2. **Racial and Social Equality:**  
    - Stood against systemic racism and segregation, calling for equal rights and economic opportunities for African Americans and marginalized communities.  

3. **Anti-War Advocacy:**  
    - Opposed U.S. imperialist interventions and militarism, particularly during the Vietnam War and Iraq War.  

4. **Economic Reform:**  
    - Promotes policies aimed at wealth redistribution, universal healthcare, and free education.  

---

## Challenges and Controversies

1. **McCarthyism and Government Persecution:**  
    - During the 1950s Red Scare, the CPUSA was heavily targeted by the FBI under J. Edgar Hoover’s leadership, leading to arrests and the suppression of its activities.  
    - Many Black members faced dual persecution due to their race and political affiliations.

2. **Internal Divisions:**  
    - Ideological splits over Stalinism, Soviet influence, and approaches to race and class issues caused rifts within the party.  

3. **Declining Membership:**  
    - The party's influence diminished significantly after the fall of the Soviet Union, though it remains active in progressive movements today.  

---

## Impact & Legacy

The CPUSA played a critical role in shaping American labor and civil rights history by advocating for economic and racial justice. Despite facing government suppression and social stigma, the party’s commitment to worker solidarity and anti-racist activism left an enduring mark on progressive politics in the U.S.

**Africana Studies Relation:**  
CPUSA's efforts to combat racial oppression and economic inequality are deeply intertwined with Africana Studies. The party’s work with African American leaders and organizations highlights the intersection of race, class, and social justice struggles.
