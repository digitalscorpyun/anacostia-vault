# 15th Amendment

**Type:** Legal Document  
**Date Created:** 2025-01-26  
**Last Updated:** 2025-01-26  

## Overview

The **15th Amendment** to the United States Constitution, ratified on **February 3, 1870**, granted African American men the right to vote by prohibiting federal and state governments from denying suffrage based on "race, color, or previous condition of servitude." It was a pivotal achievement of the **Reconstruction Era**, aiming to enfranchise formerly enslaved people and expand democracy in the U.S.

---

## Key Details

- **Ratified:** February 3, 1870  
- **Text:**  
  - *"The right of citizens of the United States to vote shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any State on account of race, color, or previous condition of servitude."*  
  - *"The Congress shall have power to enforce this article by appropriate legislation."*  
- **Purpose:**  
  - To ensure African American men had the legal right to vote.  
  - To expand political representation and participation of newly freed individuals in post-Civil War America.  
  - To address discriminatory voting laws passed in the Southern states.

---

## Context and Background

- The amendment was part of the broader **[[Reconstruction Era]]** efforts to provide civil and political rights to formerly enslaved African Americans following the **[[Civil War]]**.  
- Despite its passage, Southern states implemented obstacles such as:  
  - **[[Black Codes]]**  
  - **Poll taxes**  
  - **Literacy tests**  
  - **Grandfather clauses**  
  - **Intimidation and violence** by groups such as the **Ku Klux Klan (KKK)** to disenfranchise Black voters.  
- The amendment was a direct response to the limitations of the **[[14th Amendment]]**, which granted citizenship but did not explicitly protect voting rights.

---

## Enforcement and Challenges

1. **Post-Ratification Obstacles:**  
   - The systematic suppression of Black voters through state laws and white supremacist violence.  
   - The rise of Jim Crow laws that effectively nullified Black suffrage in many Southern states.  

2. **Civil Rights Movement:**  
   - The **[[Voting Rights Act of 1965]]** was passed to enforce the protections guaranteed under the 15th Amendment by outlawing discriminatory voting practices.  
   - Legal victories in the 20th century, such as *Shelby County v. Holder (2013)*, affected enforcement mechanisms and protections.  

3. **Modern-Day Issues:**  
   - Continued debates about voter suppression, ID laws, gerrymandering, and disenfranchisement of formerly incarcerated individuals disproportionately affecting African Americans.  
   - The amendment continues to be cited in legal challenges regarding voting rights and discrimination.

---

## Impact and Legacy

1. **Political Empowerment:**  
   - Allowed African Americans to hold public office at local, state, and federal levels during Reconstruction.  
   - Laid the foundation for the Black political activism seen in the 20th and 21st centuries.  

2. **Civil Rights Expansion:**  
   - Provided legal grounds for future voting rights legislation and movements, including efforts by figures such as **[[Frederick Douglass]]**, **[[W.E.B. Du Bois]]**, and **[[Martin Luther King Jr.]]**.  

3. **Supreme Court Interpretations:**  
   - Landmark cases have shaped how the amendment is applied in contemporary legal challenges related to electoral policies and redistricting.
