# Africana Studies - ASNLH🏛️📚

**Type:** Organization  


---

## Overview 🏛️  
The **Association for the Study of Negro Life and History (ASNLH)** was founded in **1915** by historian **Carter G. Woodson**. It played a pivotal role in documenting and preserving Black history, leading to the establishment of **Black History Month**.

---

## Key Details 📝  
- **Full Name:** Association for the Study of Negro Life and History (ASNLH)  
- **Founder:** Carter G. Woodson  
- **Founded:** September 9, 1915  
- **Headquarters:** Washington, D.C.  
- **Significance:**  
  - One of the first scholarly organizations dedicated to African American history.  
  - Instrumental in establishing **Black History Week (1926)**, which later became **Black History Month (1976)**.  
  - Encouraged the collection and study of Black historical records.  

---

## Timeline 📅  
- **1915:** ASNLH founded by Carter G. Woodson.  
- **1916:** Launch of *The Journal of Negro History*.  
- **1926:** Initiated **Negro History Week**, precursor to Black History Month.  
- **1976:** Negro History Week expanded into **Black History Month**.  
- **Present:** Organization continues as the **Association for the Study of African American Life and History (ASALH)**.

## Impact & Legacy 🌍  
ASNLH laid the foundation for **institutionalized Black history research and education**. It empowered African Americans to reclaim their history, counteract racial misrepresentations, and advocate for historical accuracy in academia.

**Africana Studies Relation:**  
ASNLH significantly shaped the **Black intellectual tradition**, promoting historical consciousness and cultural pride. Its legacy continues through the modern **Association for the Study of African American Life and History (ASALH)**.
