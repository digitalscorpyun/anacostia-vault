# Jemar Tisby

## Overview
- African American historian, author, and advocate focused on **race, religion, and social justice**.
- Bridges academic scholarship with activism, urging institutions (especially the Christian church) to confront systemic racism.

---

## Background
### Early Life & Education
- Raised in a multiracial Christian household in Chicago.
- **Education**:
  - **B.A. in History** (University of Notre Dame)
  - **M.Div.** (Reformed Theological Seminary, Orlando)
  - **Ph.D. in History** (University of Mississippi)  
    - Dissertation: Race, religion, and social movements in the 20th-century U.S. South.

---

## Career & Advocacy
### Key Contributions
- **The Witness: A Black Christian Collective**  
  - Co-founded platform for Black Christian perspectives on race, justice, and faith.
- **Reformed African American Network (RAAN)**  
  - Co-created to build community among Black Christians.
- **Public Speaking**: Frequent speaker at churches, universities, and conferences.

### Activism Focus
- Challenges the **Christian church** to acknowledge historical complicity in racism.
- Advocates for **racial reconciliation** through education, repentance, and systemic change.

---

## Major Works
### 1. *The Color of Compromise* (2019)  
- **Thesis**: American churches actively perpetuated racism from slavery to modern times.
- Key themes:  
  - Church support for segregation and Jim Crow.  
  - Call for institutional repentance and equity-focused reforms.

### 2. *How to Fight Racism* (2021)  
- **ARC Framework**:  
  - **Awareness** (educate on racial history)  
  - **Relationships** (build cross-cultural solidarity)  
  - **Commitment** (pursue systemic change)  
- Combines theology with actionable anti-racism strategies.

---

## Key Themes
- **Historical Truth-Telling**: Exposes systemic racism in religious and societal institutions.
- **Faith-Driven Justice**: Urges Christians to align theology with anti-racist action.
- **Education & Dialogue**: Emphasizes honest conversations about race and reparative justice.
