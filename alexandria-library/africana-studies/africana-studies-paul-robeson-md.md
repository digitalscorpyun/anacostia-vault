# Paul Robeson

**Type:** Person  
**Date Created:** 2025-01-26

## Overview

**Paul Robeson** (April 9, 1898 – January 23, 1976) was a renowned African-American singer, actor, and civil rights activist. He was known for his powerful bass-baritone voice, groundbreaking performances on stage and screen, and his unwavering commitment to social justice, anti-colonialism, and human rights.

---

## Key Details

- **Full Name:** Paul Leroy Robeson  
- **Born:** April 9, 1898, Princeton, New Jersey  
- **Died:** January 23, 1976, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania  
- **Occupation:** Singer, Actor, Civil Rights Activist, Lawyer  
- **Education:**  
  - Rutgers University (Valedictorian, Class of 1919)  
  - Columbia Law School  

---

## Timeline

- **1915 (20th Century):** Enrolled at Rutgers University, becoming one of the first Black students and excelling in academics and athletics.  
- **1921 (20th Century):** Earned a law degree from Columbia Law School but faced racial discrimination in the legal profession.  
- **1924 (20th Century):** Achieved critical acclaim in the stage production of *The Emperor Jones*.  
- **1930 (20th Century):** Starred in *Othello*, becoming the first Black actor to play the role in a major production in the 20th century.  
- **1939 (20th Century):** Recorded his iconic rendition of *Ol' Man River* from *Show Boat*.  
- **1940s (20th Century):** Became deeply involved in the Civil Rights Movement and anti-colonial struggles worldwide.  
- **1950 (20th Century):** Blacklisted during the McCarthy era for his political beliefs and connections to socialism.  
- **1976 (20th Century):** Passed away in Philadelphia at the age of 77.

## Key Achievements and Contributions

### **1. Artistic Excellence**

- **Theater:**  
  - Starred in groundbreaking productions such as *The Emperor Jones* and *Othello*, breaking racial barriers on Broadway and in the West End.  
- **Music:**  
  - Known for his deep, resonant voice and performances of spirituals, folk songs, and classical music.  
  - His rendition of *Ol' Man River* from *Show Boat* became an anthem of resistance, evolving in meaning over time as Robeson adjusted the lyrics to reflect his political views.  

### **2. Political Activism**

- **Civil Rights Advocacy:**  
  - Advocated for racial equality in the U.S. and was involved in early efforts to challenge segregation and discrimination.  
- **International Solidarity:**  
  - Supported anti-colonial movements in Africa, India, and the Caribbean.  
  - Spoke out against fascism and imperialism during World War II.  
- **McCarthy-Era Persecution:**  
  - His outspoken socialist views led to his blacklisting in the U.S., with his passport revoked by the U.S. government in 1950, severely impacting his career.  

### **3. Advocacy for Workers and Labor Rights**

- Championed labor rights and spoke at trade union events worldwide.  
- Supported striking workers and condemned economic exploitation, believing in the unity of oppressed peoples globally.  

---

## Challenges and Struggles

1. **Blacklisting and Surveillance:**  
   - Robeson was subjected to FBI surveillance and government suppression due to his political affiliations and criticisms of U.S. policies.  
2. **Career Impact:**  
   - Despite his global influence, his professional opportunities in the U.S. were curtailed by political persecution.  
3. **Health Decline:**  
   - The stress from his activism and blacklisting took a toll on his health in his later years.  

---

## Legacy and Impact

1. **Cultural Influence:**  
   - Robeson's contributions to art and music continue to inspire generations of artists and activists.  
   - His life serves as a testament to the intersection of cultural expression and social activism.  

2. **Global Recognition:**  
   - Honored in various countries for his contributions to the struggle for freedom and equality.  
   - Monuments and memorials exist in his honor in locations such as London and Moscow.  

3. **Posthumous Honors:**  
   - Received a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award posthumously.  
   - Numerous schools, theaters, and cultural institutions have been named after him.
