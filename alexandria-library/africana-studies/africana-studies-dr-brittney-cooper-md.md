## Overview  
**Dr. Brittney Cooper** is a renowned African-American scholar, writer, and cultural critic specializing in Black feminist thought, intersectionality, and race and gender politics. She is a professor of Women's, Gender, and Africana Studies at **Rutgers University**, and her work critically examines Black womanhood, feminism, and social justice in contemporary society.

## Publications  

1. **"Beyond Respectability: The Intellectual Thought of Race Women" (2017)**  
    - Explores the intellectual contributions of Black women activists and scholars from the late 19th and early 20th centuries.  
    - Highlights figures such as Anna Julia Cooper and Mary Church Terrell.  

2. **"Eloquent Rage: A Black Feminist Discovers Her Superpower" (2018)**  
    - A blend of memoir and cultural critique addressing contemporary issues faced by Black women.  
    - Examines how Black women’s rage can serve as a transformative force in fighting systemic oppression.  

3. **"Feminist AF: A Guide to Crushing Girlhood" (2021)**  
    - Aimed at young Black girls, offering a feminist perspective on empowerment and identity.
