
# Africana Studies - Joe Gans

**Type:** Historical Figure  
**Date Created:** 2025-01-27
**Last Updated:** 2025-01-27  

---

## Overview  
Joe Gans was the first African American to win a world boxing title, holding the lightweight championship from 1902 to 1908. Known as the "Old Master," he revolutionized boxing techniques with his strategic and scientific approach to the sport.

---

## Key Details  
- **Full Name:** Joseph Gant  
- **Birthdate:** November 25, 1874  
- **Birthplace:** Baltimore, Maryland, USA  
- **Major Achievements:**  
  - First African American World Lightweight Champion (1902)  
  - Known for his precise technique and tactical fighting style.  
  - Fought the legendary "Fight of the Century" against Battling Nelson in 1906.  
- **Death:** August 10, 1910 (tuberculosis)

---

## Timeline  
- **1902:** Won the lightweight championship, becoming the first Black world champion in any weight class.  
- **1906:** Defended his title in an epic 42-round match against Battling Nelson.  
- **1908:** Lost his title but continued to influence boxing strategy.

## Impact & Legacy  
Joe Gans paved the way for future Black boxers by breaking racial barriers in professional sports. His strategic fighting techniques and contributions to the sport earned him a place in the International Boxing Hall of Fame.

**Africana Studies Relation:**  
Gans' success challenged racial stereotypes and provided representation for African Americans in sports during the early 20th century, a critical period in the fight for racial equality.
