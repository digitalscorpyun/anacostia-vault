# Africana Studies - Combahee River Raid

**Type:** Historical Event  
**Date Created:** 2025-01-26  
**Last Updated:** 2025-01-26  

## Overview
The **Combahee River Raid** was a daring military operation led by **Harriet Tubman**, an abolitionist and former enslaved woman, in collaboration with Union forces during the **American Civil War**. Conducted on **June 2, 1863**, in South Carolina, the raid resulted in the liberation of more than **750 enslaved people**, making it one of the most successful operations of the war and solidifying Tubman’s legacy as a military strategist and freedom fighter.

---

## Key Details

- **Date:** June 2, 1863  
- **Location:** Combahee River, South Carolina  
- **Key Figure:** [[Harriet Tubman]]  
- **Union Leaders Involved:** Colonel James Montgomery  
- **Objective:** To destroy Confederate supply lines and free enslaved people from plantations along the Combahee River.  
- **Outcome:** Over 750 enslaved individuals liberated, Confederate resources destroyed, and a morale boost for Union forces and the abolitionist cause.

---

## Timeline

- **1863 (19th Century):**  
  - Harriet Tubman, acting as a scout and spy for the Union Army, planned and led the raid.  
  - Union gunboats navigated the Combahee River, evading Confederate defenses and destroying valuable plantations.  
  - Freed individuals were transported to freedom and enlisted in the Union Army's newly formed African American regiments, such as the **54th Massachusetts Infantry Regiment**.

## Strategy and Execution

- **Harriet Tubman’s Role:**  
  - Tubman, known for her work with the **Underground Railroad**, provided critical intelligence and guided Union forces using her deep knowledge of the local terrain and Confederate troop movements.  
  - She worked alongside Colonel James Montgomery to coordinate the attack, ensuring minimal resistance and maximum impact.

- **The Attack:**  
  - Union forces launched from Port Royal, South Carolina, with gunboats traveling up the Combahee River.  
  - Using stealth and surprise, the forces burned Confederate supplies and disrupted plantation operations.  
  - Hundreds of enslaved individuals fled to the boats, seeking freedom under Union protection.

- **Aftermath:**  
  - The raid weakened the Confederate economy in the region and strengthened the Union's resolve to recruit African American troops.  
  - Harriet Tubman earned the admiration of military leaders but was not compensated fully for her wartime efforts until later in life.

---

## Impact & Legacy

1. **Immediate Effects:**  
   - Over 750 enslaved people gained their freedom, many of whom later joined the Union Army.  
   - The raid disrupted Confederate agricultural production and supply lines.  

2. **Military and Strategic Importance:**  
   - Showcased the effectiveness of African American participation in the war effort.  
   - Proved the strategic advantage of using local knowledge and unconventional tactics in military operations.

3. **Legacy:**  
   - Tubman’s leadership in the raid cemented her reputation as not only a liberator but also a capable military leader and strategist.  
   - The Combahee River Raid remains a significant moment in African American and Civil War history, highlighting the active role of Black individuals in their own liberation.

**Africana Studies Relation:**  
The Combahee River Raid represents a crucial intersection of military history, abolitionist efforts, and African American agency. It serves as a powerful example of Black resistance and self-liberation within the broader context of the Civil War and emancipation.
