## Overview  
**Dr. Ashley D. Farmer** is an Associate Professor at the University of Texas at Austin, specializing in Black women's history, intellectual history, and radical politics. She holds joint appointments in the Departments of History and African and African Diaspora Studies. Dr. Farmer is a leading scholar on the intellectual contributions of Black women to social movements and Black radical thought.

## Publications  

1. **"Remaking Black Power: How Black Women Transformed an Era" (2017)**  
    - Examines the intellectual and activist contributions of Black women to the Black Power movement.  
    - Highlights overlooked figures and their influence on political and social movements.  

2. **"New Perspectives on the Black Intellectual Tradition" (2018)**  
    - Co-edited anthology addressing central themes in Black intellectual thought.  
    - Explores historical and contemporary perspectives.  

3. **"Queen Mother Audley Moore: Mother of Black Nationalism" (forthcoming)**  
    - A biography detailing the life and legacy of Audley Moore, a key figure in Black nationalist thought.
