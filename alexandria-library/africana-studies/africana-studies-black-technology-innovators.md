> _“If they don’t give you a seat at the table, bring a folding chair.”_ — Shirley Chisholm

---

## 📌 **Overview**

**Black technology innovators** have played a **pivotal role** in shaping modern science, engineering, and digital culture—often despite systemic barriers. From the **Industrial Revolution to the AI age**, Black inventors, engineers, and scientists have pioneered breakthroughs in **computing, telecommunications, medicine, space exploration, and cybersecurity**.

This note highlights the **historical impact, key figures, and ongoing challenges** of Black tech innovators, placing them within the broader scope of **Africana Studies** and the **global digital economy**.

---

## 🏷️ **Key Details**

- **Origins:** Black inventors have been shaping technology **since the 18th century**, despite racism and patent discrimination.
- **Scope:** Encompasses **engineering, telecommunications, AI, cybersecurity, robotics, and space exploration**.
- **Key Themes:** **Innovation, racial barriers in STEM, digital equity, AI ethics, and Afrofuturism**.
- **Influences:** African American inventors, African diaspora technologists, and Black futurists.

## 📜 **Historical Timeline of Black Technology Innovation**

📍 **1721** – **Onesimus** introduces smallpox inoculation to America, revolutionizing medicine.  
📍 **1831** – **Nat Turner’s Revolt** uses coded messaging and surveillance tactics.  
📍 **1870s-1890s** – Black inventors develop **improvements in electric circuits, railroads, and refrigeration**.  
📍 **1923** – **Garrett Morgan** patents the modern **traffic signal**.  
📍 **1944** – **Dr. Marie Van Brittan Brown** patents the **home security system**.  
📍 **1956** – **Gladys West** lays mathematical groundwork for **GPS technology**.  
📍 **1969** – **NASA’s Hidden Figures (Katherine Johnson, Dorothy Vaughan, Mary Jackson)** help land the first astronauts on the moon.  
📍 **1990s-Present** – Black scientists and tech entrepreneurs innovate in **AI, blockchain, space exploration, and cybersecurity**.

## ✊🏾 **Major Contributions & Breakthroughs by Black Innovators**

### **1️⃣ Early Black Inventors & Engineers (18th–19th Century)**

- **Benjamin Banneker (1731–1806)** – Mathematician, astronomer, and early clockmaker.
- **Elijah McCoy (1844–1929)** – Invented automatic **lubrication systems for trains**.
- **Granville Woods (1856–1910)** – Developed early **telegraph & electric railway systems**.
- **Madam C.J. Walker (1867–1919)** – Built a beauty & tech empire using **manufacturing innovations**.

### **2️⃣ Black Scientists in Computing & Aerospace (20th Century)**

- **Katherine Johnson (1918–2020)** – NASA mathematician whose calculations sent astronauts to space.
- **Dorothy Vaughan (1910–2008)** – Early **computer scientist**, first Black woman NASA supervisor.
- **Mark Dean (b. 1957)** – Co-invented the **IBM personal computer (PC) architecture**.
- **Lonnie Johnson (b. 1949)** – Aerospace engineer and inventor of the **Super Soaker water gun**.

## ⚖️ **Challenges & Future of Black Tech Innovation**

⚠️ **Lack of Funding for Black Entrepreneurs** – Less than **1% of venture capital** goes to Black-led startups.  
⚠️ **Racial Bias in AI & Surveillance Tech** – Black communities face **algorithmic policing & digital profiling**.  
⚠️ **Barriers in STEM Education** – Black students underrepresented in **engineering & computer science**.  
⚠️ **Lack of Recognition** – Many Black inventors' contributions are **overlooked or stolen** in history books.

🔗 **Possible Solutions:**

- **Investing in Black-Led Tech Firms & STEM Education** 📚
- **Addressing AI Ethics & Algorithmic Bias** 🤖
- **Promoting Afrofuturist Visions for Black Digital Sovereignty** 🌍
