## 🌟 **Atomic Note – The Ancestor’s Song**

This atomic note captures a single, distinct idea: African American spirituals as a vital musical tradition.

- **Definition and Scope:**  
  - Spirituals are sacred songs created by enslaved African Americans, blending African musical traditions with Christian hymns, often expressing faith, sorrow, and hope—[[music-history]].  
  - Originated in the 18th and 19th centuries, spirituals served as coded messages for resistance, such as escape routes on the Underground Railroad (e.g., “Follow the Drinking Gourd”)—[[africana-studies-underground-railroad]].  
  - Housed under `arts/music/africana-studies`—[[music-overview]].  
- **Cultural Significance:**  
  - Spirituals are a cornerstone of Black musical heritage, influencing genres like gospel, blues, and jazz—[[africana-studies-jazz]].  
  - They embody resistance, resilience, and community, often sung during labor, worship, or secret gatherings—[[africana-studies-resistance]].  
- **Africana Studies Tie:**  
  - Centers the African roots of spirituals, such as call-and-response patterns, and their role in Black liberation—[[africana-studies-african-rhythms]].  
- **Tech Integration:**
