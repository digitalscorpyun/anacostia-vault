## Overview
**Candacy Taylor** is an award-winning author, photographer, and cultural documentarian whose work focuses on the intersection of race, class, gender, and culture in the United States. She is best known for her groundbreaking research on the history of the **Green Book** and its significance to African-American travelers during the [[Africana Studies - Jim Crow Laws]] era.

## Major Works

### **1. "Overground Railroad: The Green Book and the Roots of Black Travel in America" (2020)**
- Explores the history and impact of *The Negro Motorist Green Book*, published from 1936 to 1967.  
- Combines historical research, personal stories, archival photographs, and contemporary images.  
- Examines how the Green Book provided safety, freedom, and resistance for Black travelers during segregation.  

### **2. Other Projects**
- **"The American Route 66"**: Documented the cultural and racial history of Route 66, focusing on its significance to African Americans.  
- **"The Root of Things"**: A multimedia project exploring hair salons and barbershops as cultural hubs in Black communities.
