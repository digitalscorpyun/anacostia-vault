# 📚 Africana Studies - Benjamin Griffith Brawley

**Type:** Historical Figure
## 📝 **Overview**

Benjamin Griffith Brawley (1882–1939) was a pioneering **African American literary historian, educator, and author**. He played a key role in **documenting Black literature, history, and culture**, contributing significantly to the early foundations of **African American Studies**. He taught at **Howard University and Morehouse College**, and his works influenced generations of Black scholars.

---

## 📌 **Key Details**

- **Born:** April 22, 1882 – Columbia, South Carolina
- **Died:** February 1, 1939
- **Education:**
    - **Morehouse College (B.A., 1901)**

    - **Harvard University (M.A., 1908)**
- **Professions:**
    - Literary historian
    - Educator
    - Author
- **Institutions:**
    - **Morehouse College** (Dean)
    - **Howard University** (Chair, English Department)
- **Focus Areas:**
    - **African American literature**
    - **Black intellectual history**
    - **Higher education for Black Americans**

## 📆 **Timeline**

- **1882:** Born in Columbia, South Carolina
- **1901:** Earned B.A. from Morehouse College
- **1908:** Earned M.A. from Harvard University
- **1912:** Began teaching at Howard University
- **1913:** Published _A Short History of the American Negro_
- **1918:** Published _The Negro in Literature and Art in the United States_
- **1921:** Published _A Social History of the American Negro_
- **1935:** Published _Early Negro American Writers_
- **1939:** Passed away on February 1

---

## 📚 **Major Works**

1️⃣ _A Short History of the American Negro_ (1913) – **Early textbook on Black history**  
2️⃣ _The Negro in Literature and Art in the United States_ (1918) – **One of the first studies of African American literary contributions**  
3️⃣ _A Social History of the American Negro_ (1921) – **Comprehensive social, economic, and political history of Black Americans**  
4️⃣ _Early Negro American Writers_ (1935) – **Biographies of early Black writers like Phillis Wheatley and Jupiter Hammon**  
5️⃣ _Paul Laurence Dunbar: Poet of His People_ (1936) – **Biography of one of the first major Black poets**

## 🌍 **Impact & Legacy**

📖 **Early Historian of Black Literature** – One of the **first scholars to systematically document African American literary contributions**.  
🎓 **Pioneer in Black Higher Education** – Helped shape **curricula at HBCUs** and promoted **Black intellectual history**.  
🖊 **Influence on Future Scholars** – Inspired later Black literary critics like **Sterling A. Brown, Henry Louis Gates Jr., and Houston A. Baker Jr.**.

**Africana Studies Relation:**  
Brawley’s work laid the foundation for **African American literary criticism and Black Studies programs**, helping to validate **Black literature and history** as legitimate fields of academic study.
