# Annie Turnbo Malone (1869–1957)

**Type:** Person  
**Date Created:** 2025-01-26  
**Last Updated:** 2025-01-26  

## Overview
**Annie Turnbo Malone** (1869–1957) was a pioneering African American entrepreneur, philanthropist, and inventor who became one of the first Black women millionaires in the United States. She revolutionized the beauty and haircare industry for Black women and built a business empire that empowered thousands of African American women economically.

---

## Key Details

- **Full Name:** Annie Minerva Turnbo Malone  
- **Born:** August 9, 1869, Metropolis, Illinois, USA  
- **Died:** May 10, 1957, Chicago, Illinois, USA  
- **Occupation:** Entrepreneur, Philanthropist, Inventor  
- **Known For:** Founder of the **Poro Company**, philanthropy, pioneering the Black beauty industry

## Career Achievements

### **1. Founder of Poro Company**
- Established the **Poro Company**, specializing in haircare products for Black women.  
- Developed and marketed the popular **"Wonderful Hair Grower,"** a scalp treatment that gained nationwide acclaim.  
- Her business empire provided job opportunities for thousands of Black women as **Poro Agents**, granting them financial independence.  

### **2. Innovative Business Model**
- Introduced a **direct sales model**, training women to distribute and promote products in their communities, predating later business strategies by major beauty companies.  
- Pioneered the use of branding and professional beauty education targeted at Black consumers.  

### **3. Philanthropy and Community Impact**
- Founded **Poro College**, which provided beauty education, business training, and a cultural center for African Americans.  
- Generously supported African American institutions, including:  
  - **Howard University**  
  - **Tuskegee Institute**  
  - **NAACP**  
- Supported Black communities during the Great Migration by funding housing and employment initiatives.  

---

## Rivalry with Madam C.J. Walker
- Malone mentored **Sarah Breedlove**, who would later become known as **Madam C.J. Walker**, one of the most famous Black entrepreneurs.  
- Despite Walker's eventual prominence, both women played crucial roles in advancing the Black beauty industry and empowering African American women economically.  

---

## Challenges and Legacy

### **1. Legal and Financial Struggles**
- Malone encountered legal disputes and financial difficulties during the 1920s, including tax issues and economic strain from the Great Depression.  
- Despite setbacks, she continued to invest in community welfare and business initiatives.  

### **2. Enduring Influence**
- Her contributions paved the way for future Black-owned beauty businesses and entrepreneurial success stories.  
- Remains a symbol of resilience, innovation, and the pursuit of economic independence for African Americans.  

---

## Impact & Legacy

Annie Turnbo Malone's contributions extended far beyond business success. She pioneered economic empowerment for African American women, set the foundation for the Black beauty industry, and provided resources for community advancement. Her legacy continues to inspire entrepreneurs and advocates for social and economic justice.
