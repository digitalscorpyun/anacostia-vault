# 🏛️ John D. Rockefeller, Jr. (1874–1960) #Philanthropy #RockefellerFamily

## **👶 Early Life and Education**

- **Birth:** January 29, 1874, in Cleveland, Ohio.
- **Parents:** Son of John D. Rockefeller, founder of Standard Oil, and Laura Spelman Rockefeller.
- **Education:** Graduated from Brown University in 1897 with a B.A. degree.

## **👪 Family**

- **Marriage:** Married Abby Greene Aldrich on October 9, 1901.
- **Children:** Six – Abby, John D. 3rd, Nelson A., Laurance S., Winthrop, and David.

## **💼 Career and Philanthropy**

- **Philanthropic Contributions:** Donated over $537 million to various causes, including education, religion, culture, medicine, and civic projects.
- **Key Projects:**
  - **Rockefeller Center:** Financed and developed this iconic complex in New York City.
  - **Colonial Williamsburg:** Funded the restoration of this historic area in Virginia, investing around $56 million to recreate the 18th-century environment.
  - **United Nations Headquarters:** Donated the land along the East River in Manhattan for its site.

## **🌳 Conservation Efforts**

- **National Parks:** Contributed to the establishment and expansion of several parks, including Acadia, Great Smoky Mountains, and Shenandoah.
- **Land Donations:** Provided significant land donations for public use and preservation.

## **🏛️ Legacy**

- **Impact:** John D. Rockefeller, Jr.'s philanthropic endeavors have left a lasting imprint on American society, influencing areas such as education, conservation, and international relations.
- **Family Continuation:** His children continued his legacy, engaging in various philanthropic and public service roles.
