# 🏛️ Politics - Andrew Jackson  

## 📝 Overview  
Andrew Jackson (**March 15, 1767 – June 8, 1845**) was the **7th President of the United States (1829–1837)**. 🇺🇸 Known as the **“People’s President”**, he expanded executive power, shaped **Jacksonian Democracy**, and led controversial policies such as the **Indian Removal Act** and the **Bank War**. His presidency was marked by **populism, federal authority battles, and economic transformation**.

---

## 🔑 Key Details  

### 👶 Early Life & Education  
- 📍 **Born in the Waxhaws region** (North Carolina-South Carolina border).  
- 🏛️ **Orphaned** at 14 during the **American Revolutionary War**.  
- ⚖️ **Became a lawyer** and settled in **Nashville, Tennessee**.  
- 🏡 **Became a wealthy landowner and slaveholder**.  

### ⚔️ Military Career  
- 🏆 **War of 1812 Hero**: Defeated the British at the **Battle of New Orleans (1815)**.  
- 🔥 **Led campaigns against Native American tribes**, including:  
  - **Creek War (1813–1814)** → Expanded U.S. land in the Southeast.  
  - **First Seminole War (1817–1818)** → Seized Florida from Spain.  

### 🏛️ Political Career  
- 📜 **Congressman (1796), Senator (1797, 1823–1825), and Governor of Florida Territory (1821)**.  
- 🎭 **Lost the 1824 election due to the "Corrupt Bargain"**.  
- ✅ **Won the 1828 election**, becoming the first **"common man" president**.  

### 🏛️ Presidency (1829–1837)  
Jackson’s presidency was defined by **major conflicts and policies**:  

#### 📜 **Indian Removal Act (1830)** 🏹  
- **Forced relocation of Native American tribes** → led to the **Trail of Tears**.  

#### 🏦 **The Bank War** 💰  
- Vetoed the **Second Bank of the U.S.**, viewing it as **corrupt and elitist**.  
- 🏛️ **Redirected federal funds** to state banks (**"Pet Banks"**).  

#### ⚖️ **Nullification Crisis (1832–1833)** ⚡  
- 💥 South Carolina **attempted to nullify federal tariffs** and threatened secession.  
- 🇺🇸 Jackson **asserted federal authority** and **prevented disunion**.  

#### 🛠️ **Expansion of Presidential Power**  
- ❌ Used the **veto** more than any president before him.  
- 🚧 Opposed **federal funding for internal improvements** (e.g., roads, canals).  

### ❤️ Personal Life  
- 💍 Married **Rachel Donelson Robards** in 1791 (later remarried in 1794 due to legal issues).  
- 👶 Adopted multiple children, including **Lyncoya, a Creek orphan**.

## 🌎 Impact & Legacy  
- 📜 **Jacksonian Democracy** expanded voting rights for **white men**.  
- 🚷 **Indian policies** resulted in **mass displacement and suffering**.  
- 💰 **His opposition to the national bank** shaped U.S. economic policy.  
- 🏛️ **Strengthened the presidency** and set a precedent for executive authority.
