#Trump_Family #US_History #US_Politics 
**Friedrich Trump** (1869–1918) was a German-American entrepreneur and the grandfather of former U.S. President Donald Trump. He is known for his ventures in the hospitality industry during the [[Klondike Gold Rush]], which laid the foundation for the Trump family's wealth.

---

### **Early Life**

- **Full Name**: Friedrich Trump
- **Born**: March 14, 1869, Kallstadt, Kingdom of Bavaria (now Germany)
- **Parents**: Christian Johannes Trump and Katherina Kober, farmers in the Rhineland region.
- **Immigration to the U.S.**:
    - Emigrated to the United States in 1885 at the age of 16 to avoid mandatory military service.
    - Settled in New York City and trained as a barber.

---

### **Key Contributions**

#### **1. Business Ventures in America**

- **Seattle Enterprises (1891-1894)**:
    
    - Moved to Seattle, Washington, and opened the _Dairy Restaurant_ in the city's red-light district, offering food, liquor, and rooms for customers.
    - Sold the business and sought new opportunities in mining towns.
- **Klondike Gold Rush (1897-1901)**:
    
    - Traveled to Bennett, British Columbia, during the gold rush and opened the _Arctic Restaurant and Hotel_, which catered to miners and offered lodging, food, and entertainment.
    - Capitalized on the influx of prospectors, amassing significant wealth through services rather than mining itself.
- **Monte Cristo, Washington (1894-1897)**:
    
    - Operated hotels and restaurants serving miners in the boomtown of Monte Cristo.
    - Left the area when mining prospects declined.

---

#### **2. Return to Germany and Family Legacy**

- **Marriage and Attempted Repatriation**:
    
    - Returned to Germany in 1901 and married Elisabeth Christ.
    - Attempted to regain German citizenship but was denied due to his earlier evasion of military service, leading the couple to settle in New York.
- **Establishing the Trump Family in the U.S.**:
    
    - Invested in real estate in the Queens borough of New York City, buying land and developing rental properties.
    - Laid the groundwork for what would later become the Trump family real estate empire.

---

### **Philosophy and Approach**

- Trump was known for his entrepreneurial acumen, capitalizing on frontier opportunities and taking calculated risks in growing economies.
- He believed in leveraging hospitality and real estate to cater to transient populations and build lasting wealth.

---

### **Legacy**

1. **Foundation of the Trump Real Estate Business**:
    
    - Friedrich Trump's investments in real estate in Queens helped pave the way for his son, Fred Trump, to expand the family's property holdings.
    - His business acumen and risk-taking approach influenced later generations.
2. **Historical Impact**:
    
    - His story represents the archetype of immigrant success during the Gilded Age, reflecting the opportunities and challenges faced by German immigrants in America.
3. **Recognition and Posthumous Influence**:
    
    - While not widely known during his lifetime, Friedrich’s entrepreneurial ventures are now viewed as critical to the Trump family’s legacy.

---
