## 📝 Overview  
The **National Bank Controversy** was a long-running economic and political debate in U.S. history centered on the role of a **centralized banking system**. It spanned from **Alexander Hamilton’s First Bank of the United States (1791)** to **Andrew Jackson’s veto of the Second Bank of the U.S. (1832)**, shaping American fiscal policy, government power, and economic stability.  

At its core, the controversy reflected tensions between:
- **Federal vs. State Power** ⚖️  
- **Centralized vs. Decentralized Banking** 🏦  
- **Wealthy Elites vs. The "Common Man"** 👨‍🌾  

---

## 🔑 Key Issues in the Debate  

### 🏛️ **First Bank of the United States (1791–1811)**  
- Proposed by **Alexander Hamilton** to stabilize U.S. finances.  
- **Opposed by Thomas Jefferson** & Democratic-Republicans, who saw it as **unconstitutional** and favoring elites.  
- **Charter expired in 1811**, leading to financial instability.  

### 💰 **Second Bank of the United States (1816–1836)**  
- Chartered after the **War of 1812** to restore economic order.  
- Became a major player in **regulating credit and currency**.  
- Led by **Nicholas Biddle**, who clashed with President Andrew Jackson.  

### ⚔️ **Jackson’s Bank War (1832–1836)**  
- **Andrew Jackson vetoed** the recharter of the **Second Bank**, calling it a tool of the wealthy.  
- Removed federal funds and placed them in **"Pet Banks"** (state banks).  
- Led to the **Panic of 1837**, a financial crisis that devastated the economy.

## 🔗 Related People & Concepts  
- **Alexander Hamilton** – Advocate for a strong national bank.  
- **Thomas Jefferson** – Opponent of centralized banking.  
- **Nicholas Biddle** – President of the Second Bank, fought Jackson.  
- **Andrew Jackson** – Opposed the Bank, led the **Bank War**.  
- **Specie Circular (1836)** – Jackson’s gold/silver currency policy, worsening the **Panic of 1837**.  

---

## 🌎 Impact & Legacy  

### ✅ **Supporters of National Banks Argued:**  
✔️ **Provided financial stability** for the country.  
✔️ **Issued uniform currency** to prevent inflation.  
✔️ **Regulated state banks** to prevent reckless lending.  

### ❌ **Opponents (States’ Rights Advocates) Argued:**  
❌ **Favored the wealthy elite**, hurting small farmers.  
❌ **Violated states' rights**, giving too much power to the federal government.  
❌ **Led to financial crises**, like the Panic of 1837.

## 🌍 Africana Studies Relation  
- **📌 Economic Disruption & Enslaved People:** The Panic of 1837 hurt **enslaved people and Black communities**, as falling cotton prices led to the **forced sale of enslaved labor**.  
- **📌 Banking Access:** The destruction of the national bank reinforced **exclusion of Black Americans from banking systems**, delaying financial empowerment.  
- **📌 Racialized Economics:** Many Jacksonian policies were rooted in favoring **white settlers, small farmers, and slaveholders**, while limiting economic access for Black communities.
