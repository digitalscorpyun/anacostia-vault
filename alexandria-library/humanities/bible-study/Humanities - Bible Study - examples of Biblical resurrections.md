#Bible_Study #resurrection
**Who Was Resurrected in the Old Testament?**

1. **The Widow’s Son (1 Kings 17:17-24):** Elijah revives a widow’s son, showcasing God's power and affirming Elijah’s prophetic role.
2. **The Shunammite Woman’s Son (2 Kings 4:18-37):** Elisha raises the boy through persistent prayer, emphasizing God’s compassion and faithfulness.
3. **The Man Revived by Elisha’s Bones (2 Kings 13:20-21):** A dead man comes back to life after touching Elisha’s bones, highlighting the prophet’s anointing.

**Who Was Resurrected in the New Testament?**

1. **Widow’s Son at Nain (Luke 7:11-17):** Jesus raises a young man, demonstrating his compassion and divine authority.
2. **Jairus’ Daughter (Mark 5:21-43):** Jesus restores a child to life, reinforcing the importance of faith.
3. **Lazarus (John 11:1-44):** Jesus raises his close friend after four days, signifying his power over death and foreshadowing his own resurrection.
4. **Tabitha (Acts 9:36-42):** Peter revives a beloved disciple, showing the continued work of the Spirit through the apostles.
5. **Eutychus (Acts 20:7-12):** Paul brings a young man back to life after a fatal fall, continuing Christ’s legacy through the early church.
