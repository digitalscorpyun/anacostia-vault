# 📜 **Claudia Sheinbaum**
## ✨ **Overview**

Claudia Sheinbaum is a Mexican physicist, environmental scientist, and politician affiliated with the National Regeneration Movement (MORENA). She served as the first woman elected Head of Government of Mexico City (2018–2023). A trailblazer in both science and politics, her work bridges environmental justice and progressive social reforms in Latin America.

## 📌 **Key Details**

- **Full Name:** Claudia Sheinbaum Pardo
- **Born:** June 24, 1962, Mexico City, Mexico 🇲🇽
- **Political Affiliation:** National Regeneration Movement (MORENA)
- **Profession:** Physicist ⚛️ | Environmental Scientist 🌍 | Politician 🗳️
- **Notable Roles:**
    - Head of Government of Mexico City (2018–2023) 🏛️
    - Secretary of the Environment of Mexico City (2000–2006) 🌱
- **Education:**
    - B.Sc. in Physics, National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) 🎓
    - Ph.D. in Energy Engineering, UNAM 🔬

## 🗓️ **Timeline**

- **1962:** Born in Mexico City, Mexico
- **1989:** Graduated with a degree in Physics from UNAM
- **1995:** Earned a Ph.D. in Energy Engineering at UNAM
- **2000–2006:** Served as Secretary of the Environment under Andrés Manuel López Obrador 🌿
- **2018:** Elected as the first woman Head of Government of Mexico City 👩‍💼
- **2023:** Resigned to run for the presidency of Mexico, potentially becoming the first female president 🇲🇽

Sheinbaum’s leadership emphasized sustainability, social equity, and gender representation. She broke political barriers as a woman in leadership, advancing climate action and urban reform in one of the world’s largest cities. Her environmental policies align with global movements toward decolonizing environmental justice and challenging extractive economic models.
