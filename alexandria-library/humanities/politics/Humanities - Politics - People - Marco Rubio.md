### Politics - Marco Rubio

#### Overview

Marco Antonio Rubio (born May 28, 1971) is an American politician and attorney serving as the senior U.S. Senator from Florida since 2011. A member of the Republican Party, he was a key figure in the 2016 presidential election, competing in the primaries against Donald Trump. He has been influential in shaping conservative policies, particularly on foreign affairs, immigration, and economic issues.

---

### Key Details

#### Early Life & Education

- Born in Miami, Florida, to Cuban immigrant parents.
- Raised in a working-class family with strong ties to Cuban-American culture.
- Attended Tarkio College and later transferred to the University of Florida, earning a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science (1993).
- Obtained a Juris Doctor from the University of Miami School of Law (1996).

#### Political Career

- **Florida House of Representatives (2000–2008)**
    
    - Served as Speaker of the Florida House from 2006 to 2008.
    - Advocated for tax reform and conservative economic policies.
- **U.S. Senate (2011–Present)**
    
    - Elected in 2010 as part of the Tea Party movement.
    - Member of the Senate Foreign Relations Committee, focusing on Latin America and China policy.
    - Co-sponsored the _Border Security, Economic Opportunity, and Immigration Modernization Act_ in 2013 but later distanced himself from it.
- **2016 Presidential Campaign**
    
    - Entered the Republican primary but lost momentum after clashes with Donald Trump.
    - Suspended his campaign in March 2016 after losing the Florida primary.
- **Post-2016**
    
    - Re-elected to the Senate in 2016 and 2022.
    - A vocal critic of China and a strong supporter of Israel.
    - Advocates for conservative policies on taxes, social issues, and military funding.

#### Personal Life

- Married to Jeanette Dousdebes Rubio, a former Miami Dolphins cheerleader.
- The couple has four children.
- Devout Catholic with strong affiliations with evangelical communities.

### Impact & Legacy

- One of the most prominent Cuban-American politicians in U.S. history.
- Influential voice on U.S. foreign policy, particularly regarding Cuba, China, and Venezuela.
- Once seen as the future of the Republican Party but struggled to maintain national appeal after 2016.
