# 📜 Humanities - Politics - Jacksonian Democracy  

## 📝 Overview  
**Jacksonian Democracy** refers to the political movement during the presidency of **Andrew Jackson (1829–1837)** that emphasized **greater democracy for the "common man"**, expanded **white male suffrage**, and **reduced elitist control** over government. It marked a shift away from **Jeffersonian Republicanism**, favoring **populism, executive power, and party loyalty**.

---

## 🔑 Key Principles  

### 🗳️ **Expansion of Suffrage**  
- **Voting rights extended to all white men**, removing property requirements.  
- **Increase in voter participation** (from ~27% in 1824 to 80% in 1840).  

### 🏛️ **Stronger Executive Branch**  
- **Andrew Jackson expanded presidential power**, frequently using **veto power**.  
- The **Spoils System** rewarded political supporters with government jobs.  

### 🏦 **Opposition to the National Bank**  
- Jackson **vetoed the recharter of the Second Bank of the U.S. (1832)**.  
- 🏛️ **Redirected federal funds** to state banks (**"Pet Banks"**), leading to economic instability.  

### 🇺🇸 **Manifest Destiny & Expansion**  
- Encouraged **westward expansion**, often at the expense of **Native American lands**.  
- **Indian Removal Act (1830)** led to the **Trail of Tears (1838–1839)**.  

### 📉 **Economic Policies**  
- Jackson **ended the national debt** but also caused financial turmoil.  
- The **Specie Circular (1836)** required land purchases in gold/silver, leading to the **Panic of 1837**.

## 🔗 Related People & Concepts  
- **Andrew Jackson** – **Founder of Jacksonian Democracy**.  
- **Martin Van Buren** – Jackson's political ally and successor.  
- **Whig Party** – Opposed Jackson's policies, advocating for a strong Congress.  
- **Henry Clay** – Jackson's rival, supported the national bank.  

---

## 🌎 Impact & Legacy  
- **Led to the rise of the modern Democratic Party**.  
- **Expanded voter rights (for white men) but excluded women & Black Americans**.  
- **Set precedents for strong executive power & populism**.  
- **Caused economic instability**, leading to long-term debates over government intervention.  

---

## 🌍 Africana Studies Relation  
- **🔗 Black Disenfranchisement**: While white male suffrage expanded, **Black men were stripped of voting rights** in many states.  
- **🔗 Indian Removal Impact**: Enslaved Black people in Native American nations also faced forced relocation.  
- **🔗 Strengthened Pro-Slavery Policies**: Jacksonian Democrats opposed **abolitionist movements** and supported **slaveholder interests**.
