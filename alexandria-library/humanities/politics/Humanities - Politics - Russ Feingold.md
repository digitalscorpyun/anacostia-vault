### **Russ Feingold: Overview**

**Russell Dana Feingold** (born March 2, 1953) is an American lawyer, politician, and progressive advocate. He is best known for his tenure as a **U.S. Senator from Wisconsin** (1993–2011) and his co-sponsorship of the **Bipartisan Campaign Reform Act (BCRA)**, commonly known as the **McCain-Feingold Act**, which sought to regulate campaign financing.

---

### **Early Life and Education**

1. **Birthplace**: Janesville, Wisconsin.
2. **Education**:
    - **University of Wisconsin–Madison**: Bachelor’s degree (1975).
    - **Rhodes Scholar**: Studied at Magdalen College, Oxford.
    - **Harvard Law School**: Juris Doctor (1979).

---

### **Political Career**

#### **Wisconsin State Senate (1983–1993)**:

- Represented Wisconsin's 27th District.
- Focused on progressive issues and ethical governance.

#### **U.S. Senate (1993–2011)**:

1. **Campaign Finance Reform**:
    
    - Co-sponsored the **McCain-Feingold Act** (2002) with Republican Senator John McCain.
    - Aimed to limit the influence of money in politics by restricting "soft money" donations to political parties and regulating electioneering communications.
    - Portions of the act were later struck down by the **Citizens United v. FEC (2010)** Supreme Court decision.
2. **Civil Liberties**:
    
    - The **sole senator to vote against the USA PATRIOT Act** in 2001, citing concerns over privacy and constitutional rights.
    - Championed protections for civil liberties throughout his Senate career.
3. **Foreign Policy**:
    
    - Advocated for diplomatic solutions and was critical of the Iraq War.
    - Supported efforts to combat corruption and promote human rights abroad.

#### **Post-Senate Career**:

1. **Special Envoy to Africa (2013–2015)**:
    
    - Appointed by President Barack Obama as **Special Envoy to the Great Lakes Region of Africa and the Democratic Republic of the Congo**.
2. **American Constitution Society (2020–Present)**:
    
    - Serves as the **President of the American Constitution Society**, advocating for progressive legal reforms and judicial accountability.

---

### **Notable Achievements**

1. **Progressive Leadership**:
    
    - Recognized for his principled stances, even when politically unpopular.
    - Twice named one of Time Magazine’s **100 Most Influential People**.
2. **Ethics and Accountability**:
    
    - Refused to accept pay raises as a senator and upheld strict ethical standards throughout his career.
3. **Publications**:
    
    - Author of _While America Sleeps: A Wake-up Call for the Post-9/11 Era_ (2012), discussing foreign policy and national security.

---

### **Political Philosophy**

- Advocates for **campaign finance reform**, **civil liberties**, and **progressive economic policies**.
- Strongly opposes the influence of corporate money in politics and has called for overturning **Citizens United**.

---

### **Criticism**

1. **Campaign Finance Reform**:
    - Critics argue that the McCain-Feingold Act inadvertently led to the rise of Super PACs following the Citizens United ruling.
2. **Partisan Challenges**:
    - Lost his Senate seat in the **2010 midterm elections**, a challenging year for Democrats nationally, partly due to opposition to the Affordable Care Act.

---

### **Legacy**

Russ Feingold remains a respected figure in American politics for his integrity, commitment to progressive values, and advocacy for ethical governance. His work continues to influence debates on campaign finance reform, civil liberties, and judicial accountability.
