###  J.D. Vance

**Date Created**: `{{date}}`  
**Last Updated**: `{{date}}`

#### Overview

J.D. Vance (born James David Bowman on August 2, 1984) is an American politician, author, attorney, and Marine Corps veteran. He currently serves as the 50th Vice President of the United States under President Donald Trump, having assumed office on January 20, 2025. He first gained national attention for his 2016 memoir, _Hillbilly Elegy_, which examined the struggles of white working-class Americans.

---

### Key Details

#### Early Life & Education

- Born in Middletown, Ohio, and raised by his maternal grandparents.
- Served in the U.S. Marine Corps (2003–2007), deployed to Iraq in 2005.
- Earned a bachelor's degree from Ohio State University (2009).
- Obtained a Juris Doctor from Yale Law School (2013).

#### Career & _Hillbilly Elegy_

- Worked as a corporate lawyer before moving into venture capitalism.
- Published _Hillbilly Elegy: A Memoir of a Family and Culture in Crisis_ (2016).
- The book became a bestseller and was adapted into a 2020 film directed by Ron Howard.

#### Political Career

- Initially a Trump critic in 2016 but later aligned with his policies.
- Elected as U.S. Senator from Ohio in 2022, defeating Tim Ryan.
- Selected as Trump's running mate for the 2024 presidential election.
- Became Vice President in January 2025, resigning from the Senate.

#### Personal Life

- Married to Usha Vance (née Chilukuri), a fellow Yale Law School graduate.
- They have three children and reside in Ohio.
