# Richard Mather

## Overview
**Name:** Richard Mather  
**Born:** 1596, Lowton, Lancashire, England  
**Died:** 1669, Dorchester, Massachusetts Bay Colony  
**Occupation:** Puritan Minister, Theologian  
**Known for:** Shaping Congregationalism in New England, the Cambridge Platform (1648)

---

## Biography

### Early Life
- Born in **Lowton, Lancashire, England**, in 1596.
- Studied at Oxford but left due to his Puritan beliefs.
- Emigrated to **Massachusetts Bay Colony** in 1635 to escape religious persecution.

### Ministry in New England
- Appointed pastor of the **First Church in Dorchester, Massachusetts**, serving for over 30 years.
- Key contributor to the **Cambridge Platform (1648)**, which outlined principles of Congregational church governance.
- Advocated for strict adherence to Puritan theology, emphasizing moral discipline and church autonomy.

### Family Legacy
- Father of **Increase Mather**, an influential Puritan theologian.
- Grandfather of **Cotton Mather**, a key figure during the Salem witch trials and a prominent Puritan minister.
- Established a ministerial dynasty that significantly influenced New England's religious and political landscape.

### Key Contributions
- Writings on Puritan theology and church governance.
- Played a major role in shaping early American Congregationalism.
- Advocated for the importance of covenant theology and community-based church structures.

---

## Legacy
- Remembered as a key architect of New England Puritanism.
- His theological works and leadership influenced generations of religious leaders in colonial America.
- Contributions to Congregationalism still shape Protestant traditions today.
