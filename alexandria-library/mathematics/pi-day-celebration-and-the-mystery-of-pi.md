# 🧮 Pi Day Celebration and the Mystery of Pi

> _"Pi is not just a number; it's a journey into the infinite."_  

---

## 📌 **Overview**  
Pi (π) is a fundamental mathematical constant representing the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter. It's an irrational number, meaning its decimal representation never ends and never repeats. Pi Day is celebrated on March 14 (3/14) to honor this fascinating number.

---

## 📜 **Key Facts About Pi**  

- **Definition**: π = Circumference / Diameter  
- **Decimal Representation**: 3.1415926535... (infinite and non-repeating)  
- **Irrationality**: Cannot be expressed as a simple fraction of two integers  
- **Universality**: The ratio remains constant for all circles, regardless of size  

---

## 🌟 **Why Pi Matters**  
- **Geometry**: Essential for calculations involving circles, spheres, and waves  
- **Science & Engineering**: Used in physics, astronomy, and signal processing  
- **Mathematics**: Represents one of the most important transcendental numbers  

---

## 📊 **Visual Proof of Pi's Irrationality**  
A fascinating visual demonstration involves two balls moving in circular paths:  
1. **Rational Speed Ratio**: If the second ball moves at a rational multiple (e.g., 3.5 times) the speed of the first, the pattern eventually repeats.  
2. **Irrational Speed Ratio**: When the second ball moves at π times the speed of the first, the pattern never repeats, illustrating pi's infinite, non-repeating nature.
