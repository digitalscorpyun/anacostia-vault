## 🌍 **The Coffee Battlefield**

🔹 **🛡 Scope & Sacred Grounds**  
- **Origins & Spread**: Born in Ethiopia, coffee crossed to Yemen by the 15th century, spreading to Java (17th century) and Brazil (18th century), now the top producer (29% of 10.8M tonnes, 2022)—[[world-history-overview]].  
- **Species**: Arabica (70% of production) thrives at 600–2,000m, needing 64–70°F and 1,500–2,000mm rain. Robusta, hardier, grows at lower altitudes (24–26°C), dominating in Vietnam (18% of global supply).  
- **Soil Power**: Volcanic soils (e.g., Ethiopia, pH 5–6) boost nutrient richness—[[volcanic-soil]].  

🔹 **📚 Challenges & Resistance**  
- **Colonial Exploitation**: Coffee’s spread fueled plantation economies, often on stolen lands, echoing modern fair trade struggles—125M livelihoods hang in the balance—[[africana-studies-decolonization-of-africa]].  
- **Climate Crisis**: Rising temperatures (IPCC 2014) threaten Arabica’s range—Kenya’s minimum altitude may rise from 3,300 to 4,600 feet by 2050. Pests like the coffee berry borer, thriving in warmer climates, cost $500M yearly.  
- **Labor Struggles**: Hand-picking (e.g., Colombia’s fincas) is labor-intensive—pickers earn $1–10 per basket, often harvesting unripe cherries for cheaper blends, perpetuating inequity.  

🔹 **⚔ Royal Purple Solutions**  
- **Shade-Grown Power**: Agroforestry (e.g., Ethiopian forest gardens) mimics natural conditions, reducing pests, boosting biodiversity, and sequestering carbon—yields can rise 10–30% (Brazilian studies).  
- **Tech Resistance**: Python can model climate impacts and pest spread—[[python-studies]].  
- **Justice Harvest**: Fair trade co-ops (e.g., Guatemala’s ACODIHUE) ensure minimum prices, reinvesting premiums (e.g., €76.6M in 2018) into communities—[[roadmap-anacostia-vault]].
