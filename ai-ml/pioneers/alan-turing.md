## 📌 **Overview**

**Alan Turing** (1912–1954) was a British mathematician, logician, and cryptanalyst. He is widely regarded as the **father of modern computing** and **artificial intelligence**. His development of the **Turing machine** laid the theoretical foundation for modern computation, and his work on **codebreaking during World War II** significantly contributed to the Allied victory.

Turing's post-war work introduced the concept of **machine intelligence**, leading to the **Turing Test**, which remains a benchmark for evaluating AI capabilities.

## 📜 **Historical Context**

- **Origins & Development:** Turing developed the **Turing machine**, a mathematical model of computation that became the foundation of theoretical computer science.
- **Codebreaking & Cryptography:** During WWII, he worked at **Bletchley Park**, where he led the team that cracked the **German Enigma code**, saving millions of lives.
- **Artificial Intelligence:** In his 1950 paper _"Computing Machinery and Intelligence,"_ Turing introduced the idea of **machine intelligence** and proposed the **Turing Test** to assess whether machines could exhibit human-like intelligence.
