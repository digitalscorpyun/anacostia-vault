# Freshman Interdisciplinary Study Plan

**Overview:**  
This four-week study plan is designed for a Computer Science major with an Africana Studies focus. It integrates foundational texts and hands-on projects in **Computer Science, Math, Rhetoric,** and **Africana Studies**. The goal is to foster interdisciplinary connections—tying technical learning to AI ethics, Afrikan epistemology, and reflective writing.

---

## Week 1: Foundation

### 🖥️ Computer Science  
- **Text**: *Introduction to Computation and Programming Using Python* (John V. Guttag)  
- **Read**: Chapters 1-2 (Basics of computation and programming fundamentals)  
- **Activity**: Implement basic Python programs (variables, conditionals, loops)  
- **Reflection**: How does computation apply to real-world Black tech innovators?

### ➗ Math  
- **Text**: *College Algebra 2e with Corequisite Support* (OpenStax)  
- **Review**: Core algebra concepts (linear equations, functions)  
- **Activity**: Solve example problems using Python to solidify your understanding  
- **Weekly Challenge**: Implement a simple Python equation solver

### 📝 Rhetoric  
- **Text**: *They Say/I Say: The Moves That Matter in Academic Writing* (Gerald Graff & Cathy Birkenstein)  
- **Read**: Introduction & Chapter 1 (Understanding academic argument structures)  
- **Activity**: Write a brief critique on a current AI ethics debate  
- **Discussion**: Analyze how rhetoric shapes narratives around AI’s impact on marginalized communities

### 🌍 Africana Studies  
- **Text**: *African American History: A Very Short Introduction* (Jonathan Scott Holloway)  
- **Read**: Chapters 1-2 (Exploring early African American experiences)  
- **Activity**: Reflect on how historical context can influence modern AI ethics debates  
- **Integration**: Begin tagging AI ethics notes with Africana epistemology themes

---

## Week 2: Application & Expansion

### 🖥️ Computer Science  
- **Read**: Chapters 3-4 (Functions and modular programming)  
- **Activity**: Create reusable Python functions for text analysis  
- **Interdisciplinary Reflection**: Consider how modular thinking applies to Africana historiography

### ➗ Math  
- **Topics**: Quadratic functions and polynomial equations  
- **Activity**: Develop a Python-based graph plotter to visualize equations  
- **Weekly Challenge**: Solve a real-world problem (e.g., analyze population trends) using algebra

### 📝 Rhetoric  
- **Read**: Chapters 2-3 (Argument structure & evidence integration)  
- **Activity**: Write a response essay on decolonial computing with strong evidence  
- **Discussion**: How might rhetoric influence narratives around Black technology movements?

### 🌍 Africana Studies  
- **Read**: Chapters 3-4 (Civil War & Reconstruction era)  
- **Activity**: Research computation’s role in documenting Black history  
- **Reflection**: Consider the impact of data bias on historical narratives

---

## Week 3: Synthesis & Critical Thinking

### 🖥️ Computer Science  
- **Read**: Chapters 5-6 (Data structures & algorithm fundamentals)  
- **Activity**: Build a basic keyword extractor for historical texts to aid Africana research  
- **Exploration**: Examine AI’s role in preserving and digitizing Africana studies

### ➗ Math  
- **Topics**: Exponential and logarithmic functions  
- **Activity**: Write Python code to demonstrate logarithmic trends and their applications to AI efficiency  
- **Weekly Challenge**: Model historical trends using exponential functions

### 📝 Rhetoric  
- **Read**: Chapters 4-5 (Perspectives and counterarguments)  
- **Activity**: Respond to a critique of AI ethics in Black communities  
- **Discussion**: Reflect on how effective rhetoric shapes policy on AI governance

### 🌍 Africana Studies  
- **Read**: Chapters 5-6 (The Harlem Renaissance & Black intellectual traditions)  
- **Activity**: Archive and tag key influences on Africana studies related to AI  
- **Integration**: Investigate computational methods in cultural data preservation

---

## Week 4: Advanced Application & Reflection

### 🖥️ Computer Science  
- **Read**: Chapters 7-8 (Complexity & optimization)  
- **Activity**: Optimize an AI scraping tool as part of your Python projects for vault integration  
- **Project**: Develop an interactive tool for Africana research using Python

### ➗ Math  
- **Topics**: Matrix algebra and introductory calculus concepts  
- **Activity**: Explore the application of linear algebra in machine learning and AI  
- **Weekly Challenge**: Apply matrix transformations to image processing problems

### 📝 Rhetoric  
- **Read**: Chapter 6 (Writing with clarity & persuasive power)  
- **Activity**: Refine your AI ethics essays for clarity and publishability  
- **Engagement**: Discuss how improved rhetorical skills can drive AI activism

### 🌍 Africana Studies  
- **Read**: Chapters 7-8 (Black movements & global influences)  
- **Activity**: Write a reflective essay on AI’s impact on Africana knowledge systems  
- **Integration**: Enhance your vault by integrating Africana tagging and deep interdisciplinary links

---

## Final Integration & Reflection

- **Synthesis:**  
  Combine insights from Computer Science, Math, Rhetoric, and Africana Studies to deepen your understanding of AI ethics and cultural narratives.  
- **Vault Integration:**  
  Tag and cross-link your projects and reflections (e.g., [[ai-ethics]], [[key-movements]], [[python-projects]]) for easy reference and continuous development.  
- **Continuous Reflection:**  
  Document weekly summaries or blog posts in your vault to trace how each discipline informs your growth and work.

> _"Learning is the process of connecting knowledge across disciplines—a journey best mapped in shared insights and structured reflections."_  
> — Freshman Interdisciplinary Study Plan
