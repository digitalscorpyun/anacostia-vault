## **1. Origins 🌱**

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning have roots in **mathematics, logic, and computer science**. Notable historical milestones include:

- **Dartmouth Conference (1956):** Birth of AI as a formal field. Researchers sought to build machines capable of learning and reasoning.
- **Early ML Algorithms (1950s-1980s):** 
  - **Perceptron (1958):** A basic neural network model by Frank Rosenblatt.
  - **Backpropagation (1986):** A major breakthrough in training deep neural networks, popularized by Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams.
- **Expert Systems (1970s-1980s):** Symbolic AI systems designed to mimic human expertise within specific domains (e.g., MYCIN for medical diagnosis).
- **Symbolic AI vs. Connectionism (1980s-1990s):** Divergence between rule-based systems and neural network approaches.

## **2. Mods (Developments, Key Concepts, Methods, and Techniques) 🔄**

### **Key Concepts**

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Broad field encompassing systems designed to simulate human intelligence through reasoning, learning, perception, and interaction.
- **Machine Learning (ML):** Subset of AI focused on algorithms and statistical models enabling systems to improve through experience.
- **Deep Learning (DL):** Subset of ML involving neural networks with multiple layers, achieving state-of-the-art results in areas like computer vision and natural language processing.

### **Methods & Techniques**

1. **Supervised Learning:** 
   - Trains models using labeled datasets.
   - Common Algorithms: Linear Regression, Support Vector Machines, Decision Trees.
   - Usage: `[[bias-detection]]`, `[[bias-flag-script]]`.

2. **Unsupervised Learning:** 
   - Learns patterns from unlabeled data.
   - Common Algorithms: K-means, PCA, Autoencoders.
   - Usage: Clustering historical data for trend analysis.

3. **Reinforcement Learning:** 
   - Learns through trial-and-error interaction with an environment.
   - Applications: Game AI, Robotics, Autonomous Systems.

4. **Natural Language Processing (NLP):** 
   - Techniques for analyzing and generating human language.
   - Applications: Sentiment Analysis, Bias Detection, Language Translation.

5. **Computer Vision:** 
   - Techniques for interpreting visual data.
   - Applications: Image Classification, Facial Recognition, Object Detection.

6. **Fairness Metrics & Bias Mitigation:**  
   - Tools like AI Fairness 360 and Fairlearn are crucial for evaluating AI systems.
   - Usage: `[[the-lion-of-anacostia-bias-detection]]`, `[[bias-flag-script]]`.

## **3. Current State (Modern AI/ML, Ethical Considerations, and Applications) 🌟**

### **Definitions**

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Systems designed to perform tasks traditionally requiring human intelligence, such as perception, reasoning, and decision-making.
- **Machine Learning (ML):** Algorithms enabling systems to learn from data without explicit programming.

### **Modern Applications**

- **Healthcare:** Predictive diagnostics, personalized treatment plans, drug discovery.
- **Finance:** Fraud detection, algorithmic trading, customer segmentation.
- **Natural Language Processing (NLP):** Chatbots, machine translation, text analysis.
- **Computer Vision:** Facial recognition, medical imaging, autonomous driving.
- **Generative AI:** Models like GPT-4, Claude, and others creating text, images, and music.
