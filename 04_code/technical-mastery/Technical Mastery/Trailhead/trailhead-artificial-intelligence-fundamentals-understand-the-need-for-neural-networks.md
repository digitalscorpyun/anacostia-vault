### **Updated Notes: Understanding the Need for Neural Networks**

#### **Learning Objectives**
- **Explain the limitation of AI models that only consider the weight of inputs.**
  - Traditional AI models assign a single weight to each input, which oversimplifies complex relationships. For example, in the "milk run" scenario, rain might be a significant factor on a Tuesday evening but irrelevant on a Saturday afternoon. A single weight cannot capture such context-dependent relationships.
- **Describe the role of neural networks in machine learning.**
  - Neural networks allow AI to identify complex relationships between inputs and outputs by considering multiple scenarios and interactions. They mimic human learning by adjusting weights and biases based on experience (data).
- **Define the major components of neural networks.**
  - **Nodes (Neurons)**: Represent individual scenarios or features in the data.
  - **Layers**: 
    - **Input Layer**: Receives raw data.
    - **Hidden Layers**: Process intermediate computations.
    - **Output Layer**: Produces the final prediction or classification.
  - **Weights**: Determine the strength of connections between nodes.
  - **Biases**: Adjust the output of a node to improve accuracy.
- **Describe how complexity is added to neural networks, and define deep learning.**
  - **Adding Complexity**: Increase the number of nodes and layers to capture more nuanced relationships. However, too many nodes or layers can overcomplicate the model.
  - **Deep Learning**: Training neural networks with multiple layers to uncover hidden patterns in data. It requires significant computational power but yields highly accurate results.
- **Explain how it’s impossible to interpret the weights and biases determined through training.**
  - The values of weights and biases often lack intuitive meaning. While they contribute to the model’s accuracy, their exact relevance to the input data is unclear, making neural networks a "black box."

#### **Key Concepts**
- **Neural Networks**: Mimic human learning by adjusting weights and biases based on data. They can handle complex, context-dependent problems.
- **Deep Learning**: Involves training neural networks with multiple layers to find hidden patterns in data.
- **Black Box Nature**: Neural networks are powerful but often act as a "black box," making it difficult to interpret their decisions.

#### **Examples and Applications**
- **Milk Run Scenario**: Demonstrates how neural networks can handle context-dependent relationships by considering multiple scenarios (e.g., rain on a Tuesday evening vs. a Saturday afternoon).
- **Password Reset Example**: Neural networks can recognize a customer’s intent to reset a password even without specific keywords, highlighting their ability to understand complex interactions.

#### **Visual Aids**
- **Node Connections**: Diagrams showing how nodes are connected with weights and biases.
- **Layered Structure**: Illustrations of neural networks with input, hidden, and output layers.

#### **Summary**
- Neural networks are essential for handling complex AI tasks by modeling relationships that traditional models cannot.
- Deep learning enhances neural networks by adding layers to uncover hidden patterns.
- While neural networks are powerful, their "black box" nature makes interpreting their decisions challenging.
